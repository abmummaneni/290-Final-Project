POD_022 | Up and Vanished: Season 2 | Payne Lindsey | Podcast
================================================================================
FULL PODCAST SYNOPSIS — SPOILERS INCLUDED
================================================================================

--- BACKGROUND AND CONTEXT ---

"Up and Vanished" is an investigative true crime podcast created and hosted
by Payne Lindsey, a filmmaker from Atlanta. Season 1 (2016–2017) covered the
cold case disappearance of Georgia teacher Tara Grinstead and is credited with
directly contributing to the arrest and conviction of her killer. Season 2,
released in 2018–2019, covers the disappearance of Kristal Reisinger, a young
woman who vanished in the summer of 2016 from the small, unusual community of
Crestone, Colorado.

Season 2 is notably different in texture from Season 1: where the Grinstead
case had a relatively clear social world and a case that was eventually resolved
cleanly, the Kristal Reisinger case takes place in one of the most unusual
communities in the American West — a small town that has become a magnet for
spiritual seekers, alternative lifestyles, and people who have deliberately
left mainstream society — and the resolution, while it produced an arrest, is
less complete and more troubling than Season 1's conclusion.

--- KRISTAL REISINGER AND CRESTONE ---

Kristal Reisinger was twenty-eight years old when she disappeared from Crestone,
Colorado, in July 2016. She was a young mother — she had a daughter — and she
had come to Crestone as part of the community of spiritual seekers and
alternative-lifestyle adherents who have made the town their home. Crestone
is situated at the base of the Sangre de Cristo mountains in the San Luis
Valley and has attracted, over several decades, an extraordinary concentration
of spiritual retreat centers, intentional communities, and people seeking
experiences outside mainstream American life.

Kristal was involved in the drug culture that existed alongside the spiritual
community in Crestone — she had struggled with addiction, and her life in the
period before her disappearance was unstable. She had a specific circle of
acquaintances in Crestone, some of whom would become central to the investigation.

--- THE DISAPPEARANCE ---

Kristal was last seen in Crestone in July 2016. She was reported missing by
her family, who lived elsewhere. The Saguache County Sheriff's Office handled
the initial missing persons investigation, but with limited resources and in
a community where people come and go and sometimes deliberately avoid contact
with authorities, the investigation moved slowly.

The specific circumstances of her last known whereabouts were difficult to
establish because many of the people who might have been with her were reluctant
to speak to authorities. The culture of the community — which included a general
distrust of law enforcement — created practical barriers to the investigation.

--- PAYNE LINDSEY'S INVESTIGATION ---

Lindsey brings his team to Crestone and begins his investigation in the same
format as Season 1: attending community events, interviewing locals, building
relationships with people who knew Kristal, and releasing episodes as the
investigation develops in real time.

Crestone proves to be a more challenging environment than Ocilla, Georgia (the
setting of Season 1). The community is fragmented and includes people with
specific reasons not to cooperate with outsiders. Many of the people connected
to Kristal's last days are evasive, inconsistent in their accounts, or clearly
concealing information.

The podcast captures the specific atmosphere of Crestone: beautiful, isolated,
genuinely alternative, and containing within its spiritual and intentional
community a darker current — the drug trade, the people who fall through the
cracks of a community that defines itself by its freedom from mainstream norms.

--- THE SUSPECTS ---

The investigation develops several significant persons of interest in Kristal's
disappearance. The people most connected to her final days are a circle of
acquaintances with complex relationships to each other and to the drug culture
of the area.

One figure in particular — a man named Joshua Lee Valentine, who was involved
with Kristal and who was present in her circle at the time of her disappearance —
becomes the central focus of the investigation. Valentine's behavior in the
aftermath of Kristal's disappearance is suspicious: inconsistent accounts,
evasive responses, and specific things he said to people that do not match the
story of someone who simply lost contact with a friend.

Valentine had a history of violence and criminal behavior. His specific
relationship with Kristal — the nature of their involvement and what happened
between them in the days before she disappeared — is what the investigation
works to establish.

--- COMMUNITY DYNAMICS ---

The podcast documents the specific social dynamics of Crestone in considerable
detail. The community's self-governing tendencies, its distrust of outside
institutions, and its specific culture of tolerance for unconventional behavior
created conditions in which someone could vanish and the community's first
instinct would not be to call the police.

Several community members had information about what happened to Kristal but
were reluctant to come forward — both because of distrust of law enforcement
and because of more immediate fears about the people involved. The podcast
captures the experience of people in small communities who know something
dangerous and have to calculate the risks of disclosure.

--- KRISTAL'S FAMILY ---

Kristal's mother and family members in other parts of Colorado are central
figures in the podcast. Their experience — trying to find out what happened
to Kristal from outside the community, working with limited information and
limited official assistance, trying to understand a world that Kristal had
entered that was quite different from their own — gives the podcast its emotional
grounding.

The specific grief of a parent whose child has disappeared into circumstances
they cannot fully understand, and who receives years of waiting without answers,
is one of the season's most affecting elements.

--- THE ARREST ---

In December 2018 — while Season 2 was still being released — Joshua Lee
Valentine was arrested and charged with the murder of Kristal Reisinger.
The charges were brought by Saguache County authorities. The arrest was
significant but did not immediately resolve the case: no remains had been found,
the evidence was substantially circumstantial, and the specific circumstances
of Kristal's death — what happened, exactly, and where her body was — remained
unclear.

The podcast's investigation is credited with generating public pressure and
renewed attention to the case that contributed to the arrest, though the specific
legal contribution of the podcast to the prosecution's case is harder to
quantify than in the Tara Grinstead case.

--- THE UNRESOLVED ELEMENTS ---

Season 2's resolution is notably less complete than Season 1's. While Valentine
was arrested and charged, the prosecution faced significant challenges:

Kristal's remains were never found, which created substantial evidentiary
difficulties.

The case depended heavily on witness testimony from people in a community
that was not reliably cooperative with the legal process.

The specific timeline and circumstances of the killing were not established
with the clarity that a body or physical evidence would have provided.

As of the podcast's conclusion and through the period of this synopsis's
coverage, Valentine was awaiting trial, and the case's ultimate legal outcome
was not fully resolved.

--- LINDSEY'S ROLE AND METHOD ---

Payne Lindsey's approach in Season 2 is more self-aware than in Season 1.
Having been through the experience of the Grinstead case — in which his podcast
directly contributed to a resolution but also generated criticism about the
ethics of investigative journalism that may influence law enforcement — he is
more cautious about how he presents information and more explicit about the
limits of what a podcast investigation can and should do.

The ethical tensions of investigative true crime podcasting are present
throughout: the danger of interfering with ongoing investigations, the
responsibility toward the community being covered, the specific risks to
people in a vulnerable community when their lives are broadcast to millions
of listeners.

--- SUMMARY ---

"Up and Vanished Season 2" is a solid investigative podcast whose case is
genuinely compelling but whose resolution is less complete than Season 1.
The Crestone setting is unusual and richly drawn, the Kristal Reisinger case
raises important questions about which disappearances receive attention and
which do not, and the investigation of the community dynamics around the case
is one of the podcast's strongest elements. The arrest of Valentine is a
significant development, but the absence of Kristal's remains and the
unresolved legal proceedings leave the season without the clean closure that
made Season 1 so satisfying.

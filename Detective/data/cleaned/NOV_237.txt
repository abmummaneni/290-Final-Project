Unnatural Exposure thrusts Chief Medical Examiner Kay Scarpetta into a dual crisis of copycat dismemberments mimicking an Irish serial killer and a bioterror plot unleashing mutated smallpox via contaminated facial sprays, orchestrated by vengeful virologist Dr. Phyllis Crowder seeking revenge for a career-ending lab accident. The narrative races from Dublin torsos to Virginia landfills, graphic emails from killer "deadoc," and Scarpetta's quarantine at Fort Detrick, ending with Crowder's confession and death while Scarpetta confronts grief over her late lover's infidelity.
Dublin Dismemberments and Virginia Copycats

Scarpetta lectures in Dublin, consulting on five unidentified, cleanly dismembered torsos—headless, limbless, found in landfills with Stryker saw marks—then rushes home when a Virginia landfill yields four matching partial remains. Panic mounts as TV leaks (via rival Percy Ring) spark serial-killer fears; Scarpetta spots subtle differences in saw blades, dubbing hers a copycat while briefing FBI's Benton Wesley at Quantico's CASKU.

Pete Marino, rough-edged homicide commander, clashes with Ring's grandstanding; niece Lucy, FBI tech whiz, traces "deadoc" AOL taunts—gruesome crime-scene photos on blue drop cloths, counting victims to "ten." Virtual-reality immersion reveals a stripped domestic room, not lab, narrowing the hunt.
Smallpox Emergence and Tangier Island

Landfill torso vesicles yield Guarnieri bodies, confirming poxvirus; colleague Dr. Phyllis Crowder verifies mutant smallpox (monkeypox spliced in), non-contagious but lethal. On remote Tangier Island, elderly Lila Pruitt dies pustule-covered, a Vita aromatherapy spray nearby; two more locals succumb, prompting USAMRIID quarantine and Scarpetta's isolation at Fort Detrick in a sealed biocontainment tent.

Tracer tests link sprays to tainted batches; deadoc emails escalate, mirroring Scarpetta's profile (stolen credit card), while budget furloughs hamstring response—non-essential feds sent home amid federal shutdown chaos.
Crowder's Revenge and Lab Backstory

Camper VIN traces to Maryland; Scarpetta recognizes Crowder's dining room from photos—chandelier scars, father's corncob pipe, blue walls matching evidence. Confronted, Crowder confesses: 1978 Birmingham lab accident killed photographer Janet Parker (smallpox escaped containment), ruined lead virologist's career and scapegoated Crowder, who stole her engineered virus.

Jealous of Scarpetta's prominence and denied MCV chairmanship, Crowder infected her dying mother (dismembered as copycat bait), contaminated sprays targeting Scarpetta's office and Tangier, killed a drifter intruder, all to unleash chaos and frame the serial killer. Virus proves a dud—kills host but doesn't spread; FBI arrests her as she dies in hospital, lab tech Wingo perishing too.
Personal Reckonings and Wesley's Truth

Quarantine strains Scarpetta's bonds—Marino's loyalty, Lucy's brilliance, Wesley's restrained courtship—while grief lingers over Mark James, killed in London bombing. New Year's Eve, Wesley reveals Mark's infidelity (trysting with solicitor Julie McFee at Grosvenor Hotel when IRA struck), freeing Scarpetta for love; by damp London streets, she takes his arm, declaring her feelings.

--- BACKGROUND AND CONTEXT ---

"Line of Duty" Series 6 was broadcast on BBC One from March to May 2021 —
delayed from its original 2020 schedule by the COVID-19 pandemic. It consists
of seven episodes directed by Gareth Bryn and written by Jed Mercurio, and
stars Adrian Dunbar as Superintendent Ted Hastings, Vicky McClure as DS Kate
Fleming, and Martin Compston as DS Steve Arnott, with Kelly Macdonald joining
as the season's central subject, DCI Joanne Davidson.

Series 6 is the show's final series. It was one of the most-watched dramas
in BBC history, averaging over twelve million viewers per episode in the UK,
and its finale became a genuine national television event. The audience had
been building toward the identification of "H" — the fourth man, the senior
corrupt officer — across five previous series, and Series 6 promised and
delivered that resolution.

Whether the resolution fully satisfied the audience and the critical community
was itself a subject of considerable debate, and the specific nature of the
finale is one of the most discussed conclusions in recent British television
history.

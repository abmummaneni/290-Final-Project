STRUCTURE NOTE

This novel is told in reverse chronological order. Part Two covers Day 15 back to Day 1,
meaning the reader experiences the events backwards, learning consequences before causes.
This synopsis reconstructs the events in forward chronological order for clarity.

BACKGROUND: CORINNE'S DISAPPEARANCE, TEN YEARS EARLIER


Cooley Ridge, Ten Years Ago

Nicolette ("Nic") Farrell grew up in Cooley Ridge, a small, insular town in the rural South.
Ten years before the novel's present-day action, her best friend Corinne Prescott disappeared
without a trace. The case was never solved. Several young men in their social circle were
investigated — including Nic's then-boyfriend Jackson Porter, her brother Daniel, and Tyler
Ellison — but no one was charged. Corinne's body was never found.

The disappearance fractured the group and the town. Nic left Cooley Ridge as soon as she
could, built a new life in Philadelphia as a school counselor, and became engaged to a man
named Everett. She has spent a decade not thinking about Corinne, or trying not to.


The Cavern

Central to the backstory is a local landmark called the cavern — a limestone cave system near
town used by teenagers as a party spot. It is connected in memory and in fact to Corinne's
last known movements. It will become connected to the present-day disappearance as well.

THE PRESENT: NIC RETURNS TO COOLEY RIDGE


Her Father's Decline

Nic returns to Cooley Ridge because her father has developed dementia and can no longer live
safely alone. Her brother Daniel has been managing the situation but is overwhelmed. The
family home needs to be prepared for sale to fund her father's care. Nic intends to stay a
few weeks, sort things out, and return to her Philadelphia life and her fiancé Everett.

She is immediately pulled back into the social world she escaped — reconnecting with Daniel,
running into Tyler Ellison (who is now a police officer), and crossing paths with Jackson,
her old boyfriend, who never left town.


Annaleise Carter Disappears

Shortly after Nic's arrival, a young local woman named Annaleise Carter goes missing. The
disappearance echoes Corinne's with horrible precision — same town, same circumstances,
same feeling of a community that doesn't know what happened but is certain it was someone
they know. Annaleise was connected to several of the same men from Nic's old circle: she
had a relationship with Daniel, and she had also been close to Jackson.

Tyler, as a local officer, is involved in the search. The town's fear and suspicion
immediately reactivate old wounds around Corinne's case.


Nic's Fragmented Memory

As the investigation into Annaleise unfolds — told in reverse — Nic reconstructs what she
knows and what she has suppressed about the night Annaleise disappeared. She realizes she
has gaps. She was in town. She was at certain places. Things happened that she is not
certain she fully remembers or has not allowed herself to fully remember.

THE MEN IN THE CIRCLE


Daniel Farrell

Nic's brother is a troubled, financially struggling man who has never truly recovered from
the shadow of Corinne's disappearance and the suspicion it cast on him. His relationship
with Annaleise was recent and complicated. He is secretive with Nic, clearly frightened,
and behaving in ways that increase her suspicion that he knows more than he says.


Jackson Porter

Jackson stayed in Cooley Ridge and has lived under the long shadow of Corinne's case ever
since. He and Nic have an unresolved history — the pull between them is still present when
she returns, which complicates her relationship with Everett. Jackson also had a connection
to Annaleise. He is volatile in the way that people who have been suspected of something
terrible for years without resolution become volatile.


Tyler Ellison

Tyler becoming a police officer in Cooley Ridge is its own kind of loaded detail. He was
in the original circle of suspects and now holds the badge investigating the new case. His
interest in Nic reads as both investigative and personal. He knows the terrain — literally
and socially — better than any outsider could.

WHAT HAPPENED TO CORINNE


The Old Secret

As Nic's present-day investigation into Annaleise's disappearance strips away layers, the
truth about Corinne's disappearance also finally surfaces. Corinne was not simply a victim.
She had been manipulative, dangerous in her own way — she held power over the young men in
the group through secrets and the threat of exposure. The night she disappeared, there was a
confrontation at the cavern. What exactly occurred has been held in silence for ten years by
everyone who was there.

Corinne was killed — not by a single person acting alone, but in a moment of chaos that
several people witnessed or were proximate to. The truth was collectively buried. Each
person in the group has carried a version of that night, and no single version is complete.

WHAT HAPPENED TO ANNALEISE


The Convergence

Annaleise, it emerges, had begun to figure out what happened to Corinne. She had been
asking questions, poking at old wounds, and had gotten close enough to the truth to
become dangerous to those who had kept the secret. Her disappearance is not a coincidence
echoing Corinne's — it is a direct consequence of it.

The person responsible for Annaleise's death is someone inside the old group, acting to
protect the decade-old secret about Corinne. The identity of who acted, and exactly how,
is the novel's central unraveling — revealed piece by piece as the reverse timeline moves
backward toward the moment of Annaleise's death.


Nic's Role

Nic herself is not innocent. Her fragmented memory conceals not just shock but complicity —
she was present at or near certain moments and made choices about what to do and not do.
Her reconstruction of events forces her to confront how much she has always known, or
suspected, and chosen not to examine.

RESOLUTION

The reverse structure means the reader arrives at the novel's final chapter — Day 1 — having
already experienced the consequences, making the moment of origination land with full weight.
The truth about both disappearances is exposed. The person who killed Annaleise to protect
the secret about Corinne is revealed. Law enforcement — in the complicated form of Tyler —
is confronted with what the town has been hiding.

Nic's life in Philadelphia, her engagement, her careful constructed distance from Cooley
Ridge — none of it survives intact. The novel ends with Nic remaining in the difficult,
unresolved space of a woman who has finally stopped running from the truth, at considerable
cost.

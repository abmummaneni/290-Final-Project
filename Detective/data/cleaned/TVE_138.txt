--- BACKGROUND AND CONTEXT ---

The CW's "Nancy Drew" is a supernatural mystery drama series that premiered
in October 2019, developed by Noga Landau, Josh Schwartz, and Stephanie Savage.
It reimagines the classic Nancy Drew character for a contemporary young adult
audience, setting the story in the fictional coastal town of Horseshoe Bay,
Maine, and incorporating elements of supernatural horror — ghosts, curses, and
the specific mythology of the town's haunted history — alongside the detective
mystery elements of the original character.

The series stars Kennedy McMann as Nancy Drew: a recently graduated high school
student who planned to leave Horseshoe Bay for college but has been derailed
by her mother's death and is working as a waitress at the Claw seafood restaurant
while her plans stall. She is accompanied by a group of friends and acquaintances
who become drawn into the investigation of the supernatural mystery at the
season's centre.

The show is notable for successfully combining genuine mystery plotting with
genuine supernatural horror in a way that respects both genres — the mysteries
have real solutions and the supernatural elements are real rather than explained
away. It has a loyal audience and was renewed for multiple subsequent seasons.

--- THE DEATH OF TIFFANY HUDSON ---

The season opens with the death of Tiffany Hudson — the wife of Ryan Hudson,
a member of Horseshoe Bay's most prominent family, the Hudsons. Tiffany dies
at a town event, apparently poisoned, and Nancy and her group are at the scene.
This makes them all persons of interest and draws Nancy into an active murder
investigation.

The investigation of Tiffany's death — who killed her, why, and what it reveals
about the Hudson family and their specific history in Horseshoe Bay — is the
season's central criminal mystery. The Hudsons are wealthy, powerful, and
connected to the town's history in specific ways that the investigation gradually
illuminates.

--- THE GHOST OF DEAD LUCY ---

Alongside the murder investigation, a supernatural thread develops immediately:
the ghost of Lucy Sable — known as "Dead Lucy," a young woman who died under
mysterious circumstances on a local cliff on the night of her senior prom
eighteen years earlier — begins appearing to Nancy and haunting the group.

Dead Lucy's presence is not simply atmospheric. She is trying to communicate
something — trying to direct Nancy toward something specific that she needs
to understand. The mystery of what happened to Lucy, why she is still present,
and what she wants Nancy to find is the season's supernatural arc, running
parallel to and eventually converging with the murder investigation.

Lucy Sable's death eighteen years ago is the season's oldest mystery, and the
specific circumstances of what happened to her — who she was, what happened
at the prom, and who was responsible for her death — are assembled across the
season through the combined investigation of the criminal present and the
supernatural past.

--- NOTES ON THE SERIES ---

The CW's Nancy Drew is a more sophisticated and more genuinely effective piece
of genre television than its YA network context might suggest. The combination
of genuine mystery plotting and genuine supernatural horror — handled with
internal consistency and real atmosphere — creates a specific kind of pleasure
that respects both traditions. Kennedy McMann's Nancy is a strong central
character: driven, intelligent, and given genuine personal complexity by her
specific situation. The ensemble around her is well-constructed, and the
Horseshoe Bay mythology — developed with care across the season — gives the
show the specific sense of place that the best mystery series requires.

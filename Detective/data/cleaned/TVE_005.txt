

TVE_005 – Columbo: Identity Crisis

This episode centers on the murder of a wealthy industrialist, whose death is meant to look like a suicide after a business failure. The case is set in a corporate environment where the victim’s heirs and coworkers are eager to accept the suicide narrative to protect their own interests.

The prime suspect is a junior executive who had recently been fired by the victim. The executive is found near the scene, holding a note that appears to be a confession. The police are ready to close the case, but Columbo notices that the note is written in a style that does not match the executive’s known writing sample.

The real killer is the victim’s wife, who has been quietly building a fortune through a series of secret investments that will become public only after her husband’s death. The murder is disguised as a suicide by overdose, but the overdosage is too precise for a simple accident. The pills are counted, and the timing is arranged so that the victim appears to have taken them deliberately.

Columbo’s first clue is the condition of the pills. The victim’s pills are common, but the ones found in the body are rare and controlled. The difference is slight, but Columbo notices it. The second clue is the location of the body. The victim is found in a chair by the window, but the window shade is down, which is not his habit. The shade is down to hide the fact that someone else was in the room.

The final clue is the wife’s alibi. She claims to have been at a dinner party at the time of the murder, but Columbo discovers that the dinner party guests arrived later than they say. The timeline is compressed, and the dinner is staged to make the alibi plausible. The wife’s hand, in a photograph from the dinner, is trembling, which Columbo interprets as a sign of guilt.

In the final confrontation, Columbo lays out the sequence of the murder, the staged dinner, and the rare pills. The wife, cornered by the evidence, confesses. The episode ends with the junior executive exonerated, and the wife arrested for the murder.

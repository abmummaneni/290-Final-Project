--- BACKGROUND AND CONTEXT ---

"Spiral" (French: Engrenages) Series 6 was broadcast on Canal+ in France in
2016 and reached international audiences through BBC Four in 2017. It consists
of twelve episodes and continues the show's sustained examination of the Paris
criminal justice system through a new central case while developing the ongoing
character arcs of its main figures — particularly Laure Berthaud, Gilou
Escoffier, Judge Roban, and Joséphine Karlsson — at a stage of the series
where the accumulated weight of previous series gives those arcs considerable
depth and complexity.

Series 6 is notable for the significant development of Laure's personal life
that runs through it — specifically her pregnancy, which introduces a new
dimension to the question of how someone with her specific commitment to the
work navigates the demands of that work alongside the demands of becoming a
parent. It also advances the Roban corruption investigation to a new stage
and gives Joséphine a case that tests her specific ethical position in new ways.

The investigation maps the network responsible for the murder and for the

financed and protected.

the seriousness it deserves rather than using it as simple dramatic device.
The ongoing development of Roban's corruption investigation — now in its sixth
series of accumulated complexity — continues to be one of European television's
most sustained portraits of institutional integrity under pressure. The series
represents the show at a specific stage of its development: fully mature in
its craft, deeply embedded in its characters' histories, and still capable
of finding new dramatic territory within the world it has built.

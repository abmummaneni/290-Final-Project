--- NOTES ON THE NOVEL ---

"For the Sake of Elena" is a strong mid-series George novel that uses the
Cambridge setting with real intelligence and real specificity. The victim's
deafness — the specific vulnerability exploited by the killer — is handled with
both accuracy about what deafness means in practice and genuine moral weight
about what its exploitation represents. The investigation of Elena as a full
and complex person, rather than a simple innocent, is characteristic George at
her best: the insistence that understanding a crime requires understanding the
victim in full, including the dimensions of them that the people who loved
them would prefer not to examine.

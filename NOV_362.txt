NOV_362 — Ice Cold
(Published in the UK as The Killing Place)
Tess Gerritsen, 2010
Full Plot Synopsis (Rizzoli & Isles #8)

SERIES CONTEXT

Dr. Maura Isles is a medical examiner in Boston — precise, cool-headed, and forensically
brilliant. Detective Jane Rizzoli is her close friend and professional partner — tough,
intuitive, and emotionally direct where Maura is reserved. Together they form one of
crime fiction's most durable investigative partnerships. The series allows Gerritsen
to use both a forensic and a detective perspective on each case.


━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━


PART ONE: MAURA DISAPPEARS


A Drive Through Wyoming

Maura Isles is traveling through Wyoming in winter — a road trip, a private journey, the
details of which are initially left somewhat vague. She is with a small group of people:
her companion Father Daniel Brophy, whose relationship with Maura is complicated by the
fact that he is a Catholic priest and their feelings for each other have long exceeded
what his vows permit, and a few others.

Their vehicle is caught by a sudden, catastrophic winter storm in the Wyoming mountains —
a whiteout blizzard that disables the car and leaves them stranded in the wilderness,
far from help, with temperatures dropping rapidly. They make it to a small town called
Kingdom Come, which they find apparently abandoned. The houses are intact, the interiors
preserved as if people simply stopped living there, but there is no one present.

The town is deeply, inexplicably wrong.


Kingdom Come

Kingdom Come was a small utopian religious community — a settlement built on isolated
land by a charismatic religious leader and his followers. From the outside, the town
looks like a preserved moment in time: homes, a communal building, food still on shelves.
But the residents are gone. Not fled — gone. No vehicles have left, no tracks lead out
through the deep snow, and the circumstances of abandonment suggest something sudden and
complete rather than a planned departure.

Maura and her companions are trapped there by the storm, sheltering in the abandoned
buildings, trying to understand what happened to Kingdom Come's people while also
struggling simply to survive the cold.


The Storm Deepens

As the storm worsens and their situation becomes more desperate, the group makes
increasingly disturbing discoveries. Evidence of what happened to the community is
distributed through the town — in the buildings, underground, in the surrounding landscape.
The picture that assembles is horrifying: a mass death event, connected to the community's
leadership and its theology.

Someone — or something — killed the people of Kingdom Come. And as Maura and her group
realize that the evidence suggests the killer may not be entirely gone, the survival
story becomes a horror story.


Communication and Danger

Maura's group is largely cut off from outside communication. She is able to get fragmentary
messages out, establishing that she is alive and in trouble, before communications fail.
The messages are minimal but enough to trigger the second strand of the novel.

PART TWO: RIZZOLI SEARCHES


Jane's Investigation

Back in Boston, Jane Rizzoli learns that Maura has not returned from her trip and cannot
be reached. A series of fragmentary communications confirms Maura is in danger. Jane
begins an urgent investigation to locate her — tracking Maura's route, identifying where
she was headed, and trying to find Kingdom Come before the storm buries whatever happened
there completely.

Jane's investigation runs in parallel with Maura's survival ordeal, the two perspectives
building tension from opposite ends — Maura trying to stay alive and understand the
danger she is in, Jane racing to find her and reach her in time.

Jane's instincts are, as always, first-rate, and her investigation is propelled by her
personal terror for her friend's survival. She works with her usual Boston PD colleagues
and coordinates with Wyoming law enforcement, whose knowledge of the local terrain is
essential.

THE TRUTH ABOUT KINGDOM COME


The Community's History

As Maura's investigation of the abandoned town deepens — conducted while trying to keep
herself and her companions alive — the story of Kingdom Come emerges. The community was
founded by a man with a powerful theological vision and an equally powerful need for
control. It was a closed community, cut off from the outside world, operating on its own
rules and answering only to its founder.

The founder's theology included a specific eschatology — a vision of end times and what
the faithful were required to do when those times arrived. What he required of his
followers, and what they were willing to do in obedience to him, is the core of Kingdom
Come's horror.


The Mass Death

The community died in a mass event that was part suicide, part murder — the kind of
collective catastrophe that has real-world echoes in Jonestown and similar events.
The founder led his people to their deaths in fulfillment of his theological prescription.
Some went willingly. Some did not. The physical evidence tells a story of compliance and
resistance, of people who believed and people who, at the last moment, realized what
they had given themselves to.

The bodies — which Maura eventually finds — tell this story through their forensic
signatures. Even in survival mode, in an abandoned town in a Wyoming blizzard, Maura
cannot stop being a medical examiner. She reads the dead.


The Survivor

Not everyone died. The critical complication in Kingdom Come is that someone survived
the mass death — someone who was present, who knows what happened, and who has reasons
to ensure that what happened in Kingdom Come never becomes known to the outside world.
That person is aware of Maura and her group. The storm-bound survival story becomes a
cat-and-mouse thriller with a killer who knows the terrain and has reasons to act.

MAURA AND FATHER BROPHY

The ordeal in Kingdom Come forces Maura and Daniel Brophy into an extended period of
intense proximity and stress. Their relationship — the complicated, unresolved attraction
between a secular forensic scientist and a Catholic priest — is pushed toward a point of
reckoning by the circumstances. In the face of possible death, in an abandoned town full
of the consequences of misplaced devotion, the irony of their situation is not lost on
either of them.

Brophy's faith is tested by what he sees in Kingdom Come — what religious devotion can
produce when it is cultivated by a man who uses it for control. Maura's habitual cool
rationalism is tested by circumstances that require something more instinctive and less
controlled. They are not the same people when the ordeal ends.

RESOLUTION

Jane reaches Maura — the race against time and weather is resolved. The survivor who
posed a direct threat to Maura's group is dealt with. The truth about Kingdom Come is
fully established: who the founder was, what he did, who participated, who resisted, and
what the physical evidence confirms about those final days in the community.

The survivors of Maura's group are extracted from the Wyoming mountains. The case —
the mass death at Kingdom Come and the immediate threat to Maura — is closed.

The novel ends with the personal consequences of what happened: for Maura and Brophy,
for Jane's relationship with her friend, and for the quiet but persistent question the
novel raises about the nature of faith and what separates true belief from the thing
that killed the people of Kingdom Come.

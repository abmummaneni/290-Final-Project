Unforgotten: Series 2 (ITV, 2017) — Plot Synopsis

Note on attribution: Unforgotten was created and written by Chris Lang; Andy Wilson is the director (he directed series 1–3). If your dataset attributes the show to Wilson rather than Lang, that is a labeling error analogous to the earlier Curtis Flowers case — Lang is the show's authorial mind. Series 2 aired on ITV in early 2017 across six episodes.

The series opens with a body recovered from a sealed suitcase dredged from the silt of the River Lea in north-east London. The remains are unusually well-preserved through saponification and are eventually identified as David Walker, a Conservative Party fundraiser and club promoter who disappeared in 1990 at the age of thirty-nine. The cause of death was a single stab wound to the chest, consistent with a kitchen knife blade still embedded in the body. David Walker is the principal murder victim of the series — but he is also, on the moral level the show foregrounds, a perpetrator in his own right: a serial child rapist and the organizer of an abuse network operating out of a house in Brentford in the 1970s and early 1980s, where he hosted gatherings disguised as "prayer meetings" at which he and other men sexually abused trafficked underage girls and boys. David Walker has therefore both a victim role (of the 1990 murder) and a villain role (of the underlying abuse). His motive, in the abuse, was psychopathic predatory tendencies, possibly aggravated by his own history of childhood sexual abuse by a primary school teacher (a hint the detectives initially overweight as a possible motive for his murder before discarding it). David Walker had no public concealment beyond the ordinary respectability of a successful Tory operative; privately he concealed the abuse network from his wife — though imperfectly. He was physically present at every act of abuse and at his own murder.

The investigators are uninvolved in the crime as perpetrators and serve as the series's protagonists. DCI Cassie Stuart is a senior detective in the London Metropolitan Police's cold-case unit, late forties, divorced, mother of an adult son, of middle-high social status. Her father Martin Hughes has a side plot tracking down the man with whom Cassie's late mother had an affair — uninvolved in the central crime. DI Sunny Khan is Cassie's longtime detective partner, a widowed British-Asian man of middle social status, who briefly attempts to kiss Cassie in a drunken pub scene during the investigation; both agree afterward to keep things professional. The supporting investigative team includes DC Murray Boulting, DC Fran Lingley, and DSI Clive Andrews, all uninvolved in any crime.

David Walker's family at the time of his disappearance: his wife Tessa Nixon, now in her sixties, in 2017 a serving Detective Inspector in the Metropolitan Police remarried to Paul Nixon. Tessa Nixon is uninvolved in the murder of her husband as a perpetrator — she has an alibi for the night of his disappearance and was not at the murder scene — but she is profoundly morally implicated and has been concealing for decades the fact that she knew about David's abuse of underage girls and chose to believe his lies (he told her the girls were of age and willing) in order to preserve her marriage and career. Tessa's motive is ordinary marital and professional self-protection rather than a criminal motive. Her concealment behavior is the looking-away that enabled the abuse to continue. She resigns from the police at the climax in shame. Jason Walker, David and Tessa's son, was five when his father disappeared and is in 2017 a thirty-one-year-old man still tormented by his father's vanishing. Jason is uninvolved in any crime and becomes suicidal during the investigation but is saved by the friendship of his neighbor Cath.

Three apparently unconnected adults emerge as the series' suspects, and they are the actual perpetrators of the central murder of David Walker and of two other connected, unsolved killings revealed during the investigation. They are the villains of the series, but they are villains the show treats with deliberate empathy as victims of childhood sexual abuse who entered into a mutual-revenge pact.

Marion Kelsey is in 2017 a child-cancer nurse in her late forties, married to Tony Kelsey (uninvolved in any crime as a perpetrator), of middle social status. Marion is a perpetrator of one of the three murders. Her motive is revenge for the abuse of one of her co-conspirators (Sara Mahmoud). Marion was herself sexually abused from age twelve by her father Mr. Dunphy (now deceased — see below). She has an alibi for David Walker's murder of a kind: she had publicly claimed to be in Ireland at the time, and she did move in IRA circles in her youth and once asked her IRA roommate Sinead Mary Quinn to kill David — a fact the police uncover and that initially appears to point at Marion's direct guilt before the truth of the swap is uncovered. She is concealing her IRA past from her husband, her abuse by her father from her sister Elise and her mother Joy (her mother had been told at the time by the twelve-year-old Marion and refused to believe her), and her role in the murder pact. She was physically present at the David Walker murder scene.

Colin Osborne is in 2017 a London barrister specializing in defending young offenders, gay, in a long-term relationship with Simon with whom he is in the process of adopting a young girl named Flora ("Flo"). Colin is of high social status, having previously worked as a high-flying City banker before retraining in law after a psychiatric breakdown. Colin is a perpetrator of one of the three murders. His motive is revenge for the abuse of Marion. Colin was himself sexually abused from approximately age nine by his Club Rangers scoutmaster, a man named Len Paxton. Colin has alibis built around his professional respectability. He is concealing his childhood abuse from his father Harry Paterson (who only realizes during the investigation that Colin's personality changed at age nine), from Simon, from the adoption agency, and from the police. He is also being separately blackmailed during the investigation by Flo's biological mother, who has discovered the police interest in Colin and demands £20,000. He was physically present at the murder scene of Marion's father.

Sara Mahmoud is in 2017 an English teacher and head of sixth form, applying to lead a school in special measures, a Muslim married mother of three, married to Hassan Mahmoud (uninvolved in any crime as a perpetrator), of middle-high social status. Sara is a perpetrator of one of the three murders. Her motive is revenge for the abuse of Colin. Sara was herself sexually abused as a child of approximately twelve, sold into prostitution after her mother's death in a car crash and her father throwing her out — she was one of the children David Walker raped at the Brentford house, and was treated at hospital with a dislocated jaw after one such encounter. Sara has the most carefully constructed alibi of the three: she was documented to be in Italy at the time of David Walker's murder, with photographs, dated receipts, and witness testimony. Cassie initially flags Sara's alibi as suspiciously perfect — "almost as if she knew David was going to be killed and made sure to have exonerating evidence," which is precisely the truth. Sara is concealing her past as a child prostitute (which she eventually reveals to Hassan, who supports her), the abuse, and her role in the pact. She was physically present at the murder scene of Colin's abuser Len Paxton.

The hidden relationships at the heart of the series are the connections between the three perpetrators — Marion, Colin, and Sara — who have been hiding for over twenty-five years that they ever knew each other. They met at a psychiatric unit (likely St. Helena Hospital) in their late teens or early twenties, where all three had been admitted following their respective childhood abuse traumas. They formed a pact there. Each of the three killed the abuser of one of the other two: Marion killed David Walker (Sara's abuser, the Tory fundraiser, in 1990, with a kitchen knife to the chest, and dumped his body in a suitcase in the River Lea); Sara killed Len Paxton (Colin's Club Rangers scoutmaster, who was officially recorded as having disappeared on a missing boat with the body never found, ruled a presumed suicide); and Colin killed Mr. Dunphy (Marion's father, who was officially recorded as having committed suicide by hanging despite no history of depression). The pact ensured that none of the three had any traceable connection to their respective murder victim, giving each what looked like a perfect alibi born of unrelatedness. The three have not seen each other in the intervening decades and have lived strangers' lives, building productive careers in service of vulnerable young people — a child-cancer nurse, a barrister for young offenders, a teacher rehabilitating a failing school — as conscious atonement.

Concealment behaviors are pervasive. All three perpetrators conceal both their childhood abuse and the murder pact for nearly thirty years. Tessa Nixon conceals her knowledge of David's predatory behavior. Marion's mother Joy concealed (and continues to deny) the abuse Marion told her about at age twelve. The Club Rangers and the Brentford "prayer meetings" were institutional concealments of large-scale abuse. The adoption agency is being lied to by Colin until he and Simon decide to come clean.

Other supporting characters: Tessa's second husband Paul Nixon is uninvolved; he is the senior officer Tessa eventually confesses her shame to. Hassan Mahmoud is uninvolved and supportive. Tony Kelsey is uninvolved and pressures Marion to open up. Simon is uninvolved and tries to preserve the adoption of Flo. Cath, Jason's neighbor, is uninvolved and keeps Jason from killing himself. Elise, Marion's sister, is uninvolved and is horrified to learn about the abuse from the detectives.

The resolution: Cassie reaches the breakthrough during a low moment after the drunken near-kiss with Sunny. She realizes that Sara's alibi is too watertight to be coincidental — that Sara expected to need an alibi — and theorizes that the three suspects may have swapped victims, each killing another's abuser. The team interviews the suspects' families to reconstruct childhood timelines: Colin's father Harry recalls Colin's personality change at age nine and identifies Len Paxton as the likely scoutmaster-abuser; Sunny and Elise interview Joy, who admits Marion told her about Mr. Dunphy's abuse at age twelve and was disbelieved (Elise furiously cuts her mother off). Cassie traces Marion to a country pub and observes her meeting privately with Sara and Colin — visual confirmation that the three know each other. Confronted, Colin confesses the abuse, the breakdown, the pact, and the murders to Cassie; Sara confesses to Hassan; Marion confesses to Tony.

Cassie is left with a moral choice. She has solved the case but has no physical evidence she can prosecute on — no one has confessed officially, the bodies of Len Paxton and Mr. Dunphy were never recovered, and her only witness to the meeting at the pub is herself. Pressured by her superiors to wrap up the investigation given budget constraints on historic cases, persuaded that the three perpetrators are themselves victims of monstrous abuse who have built lives of public service and will never kill again, and faced with the fact that David Walker, Len Paxton, and Mr. Dunphy were themselves serial child abusers, Cassie chooses, with Sunny's reluctant agreement, not to record her findings and to let the three suspects go free. The three killers therefore go formally unprosecuted, though privately exposed to their families. The series closes with the three perpetrators reconciling with their partners; Tessa Nixon resigning from the police in shame; Jason Walker still struggling but alive; Colin and Simon hoping the adoption of Flo can still go through if Colin is cleared; and Cassie left to live with the moral weight of a deliberate non-prosecution. The villain of the original abuse network is David Walker (deceased and unprosecutable); the villains of the murder are Marion Kelsey, Colin Osborne, and Sara Mahmoud, identified by Cassie but allowed by her to remain free.

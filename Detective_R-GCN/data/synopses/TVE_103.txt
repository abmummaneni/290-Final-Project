--- BACKGROUND AND CONTEXT ---

"Trove" is an episode from Series 5 of ITV's "Endeavour," first broadcast in
February 2018. It is directed by Shaun Evans — the actor who plays the title
role, here directing for the first time — and written by Russell Lewis, who
wrote all of the Endeavour episodes. It stars Shaun Evans as young Endeavour
Morse and Roger Allam as Detective Chief Inspector Fred Thursday.

"Endeavour" is a prequel series to "Inspector Morse," following the early career
of Morse in Oxford in the 1960s and 1970s. By Series 5, the show is in the
late 1960s and Morse is an established detective constable/sergeant working
under Thursday at Oxford's Castle Gate Police Station. The series uses this
prequel framework not simply for fan service but as a genuine investigation of
how the character of Morse was formed: what experiences, what relationships,
and what investigations shaped him into the man that the Morse series' viewers
know.

The series is notable for its production quality — the period recreation of
Oxford in the 1960s and 1970s is extraordinarily detailed — for its sustained
musical intelligence (Morse's love of opera is a central character element),
and for the depth of its portrait of the relationship between Morse and Thursday,
which is one of the finest mentor-protégé partnerships in British television.

--- FRED THURSDAY AND ENDEAVOUR MORSE ---

The Thursday-Morse relationship is the emotional foundation of "Endeavour."
Fred Thursday is a different kind of detective from the older Morse of the
original series: a practical man of the post-war generation, whose specific
moral code is rooted in his wartime experience and in the specific values of
the English working class in the 1950s and 1960s. He is not an intellectual,
though he is by no means unintelligent. He is a man of action and of specific
personal loyalty whose specific qualities complement and occasionally challenge
Morse's more intellectual approach.

Roger Allam's Thursday is one of the finest performances in the series — warm,
specific, entirely real, and possessed of a quality of authority that is rooted
in character rather than rank. His relationship with Morse has developed across
five series into something that is simultaneously professional mentorship and
genuine friendship, with the specific complications that arise when a mentor
and protégé are very different kinds of people who have come to care about each
other.

Morse in Series 5 is recognizably the person who will become the Inspector
Morse of the later series: the musical obsession, the crosswords, the real ale,
the specific intellectual arrogance, and the specific emotional unavailability
are all present and are given their contextual history through the prequel
framework. "Trove" catches him at a specific moment in that development.

Morse's investigative contribution in "Trove" is rooted in his specific

--- ENDEAVOUR AS DIRECTOR ---

"Trove" is notable as the only Endeavour episode directed by Shaun Evans himself.
His direction is characteristically attentive to the show's specific visual
qualities — the period Oxford, the specific institutional spaces, the particular
quality of the natural and built environment that the series has always used
with real intelligence. His specific knowledge of the material and the characters,
acquired across five seasons of inhabiting the central role, gives the direction
a particular authority and a particular intimacy with the show's world.

--- NOTES ON THE EPISODE ---

"Trove" is a strong Series 5 Endeavour episode that demonstrates the show at
its mature best: the Oxford setting used with real intelligence and real love,
the Morse-Thursday partnership operating with the full depth of five series'
development, and a case that is specific to both the show's world and the
period it inhabits. The combination of cultural heritage crime, academic rivalry,
and the specific period detail of late 1960s Oxford that the episode delivers
is characteristic of "Endeavour" at its finest.

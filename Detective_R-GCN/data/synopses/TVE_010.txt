TVE_010 – Columbo: How to Dial a Murder

In this episode, Columbo investigates the murder of a television producer, whose death is meant to look like a suicide by overdose. The case is set in the world of late‑night TV, where the rhythms of the show, the camera angles, and the control of the broadcast are central to the crime.

The victim is Malcolm Brand, a slick, manipulative producer who has enemies all over the industry. He hosts a popular talk show and uses his power to humiliate lesser‑known guests. The immediate suspect is a disgruntled guest, a woman who was publicly mocked on his show and later disappeared from view.

The official story is that the woman, consumed by shame, killed herself, and Malcolm’s death is a separate, unrelated incident. But Columbia notices that the timing of the two deaths is too convenient. The woman’s death is soon revealed to be a murder staged as suicide, and Columbo realizes that the same person is behind both.

The real killer is the disgruntled guest’s husband, Dr. Milton Kruger, a psychiatrist who has been quietly plotting revenge against Malcolm for years. The plan is long‑term: the guest appears on the show, is humiliated, and later dies in a car accident that is arranged to look like a spontaneous crash. The accident is caused by a tampered tire or a hidden device that makes the car veer off the road.

Columbo’s first clue is the condition of the car. The tire is cut in a way that is not natural, and the skid marks do not match the way the car would have swerved if it were a simple loss of control. The pattern suggests something deliberate, planted beforehand.

The second clue is the guest’s computer. The guest, before the appearance on the show, sent an email to Dr. Kruger expressing fears that Malcolm will ruin her reputation. The email is printed out and kept in the guest’s home, but the police miss the connection at first. Columbo finds it and traces it back to the psychiatrist.

The murder of Malcolm comes next. After the guest’s “suicide,” Malcolm is called to a meeting with Dr. Kruger, who has invited him to the doctor’s office under the guise of discussing a future guest‑appearance. During the meeting, Dr. Kruger drug’s Malcolm with a powerful sedative, then injects him with a lethal dose of a drug that mimics an overdose. The scene is staged so that Malcolm appears to have taken the drug voluntarily, with a needle left in his arm and a vial nearby.

Columbo’s final clue is the location of the needle. The angle of the needle in Malcolm’s arm does not match the way he would have injected himself. The pharmaceutical notes in Dr. Kruger’s office also reveal that the drug used is not one that Malcolm would have access to; it is a controlled substance that only a licensed psychiatrist can obtain.

In the final confrontation, Columbo explains the sequence of the first murder, then the second, and how the psychiatrist used the show’s format and the public shame to manipulate the situation. Dr. Kruger, cornered by the evidence, confesses. The episode ends with the talk‑show’s ratings still high, but the truth quietly buried: the producer is exposed as a murderer, and the psychiatrist is arrested.

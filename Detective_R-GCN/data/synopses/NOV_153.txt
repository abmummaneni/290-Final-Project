The Doomsters propels private eye Lew Archer into the fractured Hallman dynasty when escaped mental patient Carl Hallman seeks his help investigating his parents' suspicious deaths, only to trigger a manhunt after Carl's brother Jerry is shot—revealed as murder-suicide orchestrated by family doctor Dr. Gartland and Jerry's manipulative wife Zinnie to seize the inheritance amid addiction, blackmail, and buried land-grab sins.
Carl Hallman's Desperate Midnight Visit

Late-night knock wakes Archer: Carl Hallman, disheveled fugitive from the state mental hospital, begs investigation into his parents' deaths—mother drowned years ago, father (ex-Senator) recently from "heart failure." Carl claims older brother Jerry, with crooked Dr. Gartland's help, committed him to silence family secrets; fellow inmate (heroin addict Tom Rica from Archer's past) urged hiring Archer.

Archer returns Carl to the hospital, but Carl overpowers him, steals the car, sparking chaos; Jerry Hallman dies in a shootout blamed on Carl, igniting orchard-spanning manhunt across the family's vast citrus empire.
Hallman Family Rot and Inheritance Wars

Archer probes the opulent estate: widowed Zinnie Hallman, Jerry's scheming wife, eyes the fortune; her lover Gartland, the family doctor, holds compromising leverage; nurse Miss Parrish hides heroin dealings; young Eula, Carl's sister-in-law, nurses quiet resentments. Flashbacks reveal Senator Hallman's ruthless 1930s land grabs from migrant workers, original sins echoing in addiction and guilt.

Carl, junkie scion haunted by blackouts, insists innocence; Archer uncovers Jerry's death as staged murder-suicide—Carl lured as patsy.
Gartland and Zinnie's Lethal Conspiracy

Digging exposes Gartland's empire: heroin supply to Carl (via Rica), pressure-committal of Carl for inheritance control, Senator's death hastened by overdose, mother's drowning pushed by family cover-up. Zinnie, ambitious and adulterous, partners with Gartland—blackmailing Jerry, eliminating threats to seize orchards and wealth.

Archer tracks Carl through groves, confronts Gartland's web; shootout reveals Jerry killed resisting their plot, Carl framed to inherit under duress.
Archer's Breakthrough and Doomsters' Fall

Archer's epiphany—caring over convenience—cracks the case: Gartland murders Rica to silence him, Zinnie poisons Senator; Carl's "doom" stems from addiction fed by enablers, family predestined by greed. Climax sees Gartland shootout death, Zinnie arrested; Carl enters rehab, Eula inherits cleanly.

Archer reflects on toxic bonds sealing fates, his choice to intervene defying hard-boiled detachment—Macdonald's breakthrough voice of compassionate pursuit.

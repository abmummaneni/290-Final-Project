The Egyptian Cross Mystery follows Ellery Queen as he investigates a series of gruesome murders marked by beheading, crucifixion, and the tau cross symbol (T-shape), linking a rural schoolmaster, a millionaire, and a yachtsman to a vengeful killer with a limp and a hidden identity tied to family secrets and a nudist cult. The plot unfolds from a Christmas Day crucifixion in West Virginia, stalls for lack of clues, then reignites with copycat killings on Long Island, culminating in a cross-country chase triggered by a bottle of iodine that reveals the killer's true face and motive rooted in Central European vendetta.
Christmas Crucifixion in West Virginia

On Christmas morning in remote Arroyo, West Virginia, schoolmaster Andrew Van is found decapitated and nailed to a T-shaped signpost at a crossroads, his body forming a tau cross (ancient Egyptian T symbol). A bloody "T" is scrawled on his cabin door, and nearby locals recall a bearded prophet calling himself Ra-Harakht, preaching sun-worship, nudism, and Egyptian mysticism while selling patent medicines. Ellery Queen, vacationing nearby with his father Inspector Queen, examines the scene but finds no solid leads; the prophet's assistant has vanished, and the case goes cold despite the ritualistic staging.
Second Murder on Long Island

Six months later, Professor Yardley contacts Ellery about a identical crime: millionaire Thomas Brad, crucified and beheaded on his Long Island estate grounds to a totem pole forming a T. The victim clutches a red checkers piece, and Ra-Harakht's nudist cult has relocated nearby across the bay. Ellery links the cases, suspecting the "Egyptian crosses" point to the prophet or his circle; local DA Isham and gruff Inspector Vaughan assist, but tensions rise with a fistfight involving a suspect.
Third Victim and Suspect Pool

A third victim, yacht-owner Stephen Megara, suffers the same fate—crucified, headless, T-shaped—prompting Ellery to probe deeper into overlapping circles. Suspects include Ra-Harakht and followers; Brad's wife, stepdaughter, secretive chauffeur, and business partner; a mysterious English couple next door; and a man with a limp seen everywhere, possibly a disguised Central European avenger. Checkers pieces, carved pipes, fake limps, gangsters, and nudist colony antics muddy the waters as Ellery fixates on one lead before pivoting.
Iodine Clue and Cross-Country Chase

Ellery spots the overlooked key: a bottle of iodine linking the murders through victim treatments and revealing the killer's real identity beneath disguises and alibis. This sparks a nationwide pursuit, tying the tau crosses to a personal vendetta from Eastern Europe involving family honor, where the killer—posing in multiple roles—methodically eliminates targets connected by a shared past event. The ritual killings mask a rational grudge, with the limping figure as the vengeful linchpin who infiltrated both rural and wealthy worlds.
Resolution and Motive Reveal

Ellery unmasks the killer as a man driven by blood feud, using crucifixion to symbolize justice in his cultural lens while checkers and pipes provide timeline proofs. The Ra-Harakht cult proves a red herring, amplifying misdirection; the true solution hinges on family ties between victims and the killer's methodical cross-country staging. Inspector Queen stays sidelined in New York, leaving Ellery, aided by Yardley as skeptical Watson, to deliver the "analytico-deductive" triumph, though early gore and gore revelry mark the novel's raw 1932 style.

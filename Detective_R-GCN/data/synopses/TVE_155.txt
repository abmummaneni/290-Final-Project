--- BACKGROUND AND CONTEXT ---

"Spiral" (French: Engrenages) is a French crime drama produced by Canal+,
running from 2005 to 2020. Each series follows a single investigation from
beginning to end, combining the procedural work of the Paris police brigade
criminelle with the parallel processes of the French examining magistrate
system and the defence bar. The show is notable for its unflinching portrayal
of institutional dysfunction — the police, the courts, and the legal profession
are all shown as simultaneously capable and deeply compromised — and for its
sustained, psychologically rich characterisation across its long run.

Series 8, the final series of the show, was broadcast in France in late 2020
and internationally in 2021. It brings to a conclusion the long arcs of its
central characters — particularly Captain Laure Berthaud, her partner and
lover Gilou Escoffier, and Judge Roban — while delivering a characteristically
complex central investigation.

--- THE CENTRAL CHARACTERS ---

Captain Laure Berthaud leads the brigade criminelle team. She is brilliant,
driven, personally reckless, and has spent the series' entire run navigating
the tension between her commitment to justice and her willingness to bend or
break rules to achieve it. Her relationship with Gilou — her partner, her
on-again-off-again lover, and the person she is most deeply bound to — has
been one of the series' central emotional threads.

Lieutenant Gilou Escoffier is the team's most ethically compromised member.
He has been an informant handler, a man who operates in the grey zones between
police work and criminality, and whose past actions have repeatedly put him
in danger of prosecution. His loyalty to Laure is absolute; his loyalty to
the law is more flexible.

Judge François Roban is the examining magistrate whose investigations drive
the legal dimension of the show. He is principled, lonely, increasingly ill,
and has spent the series in a kind of sustained ethical conflict between what
the law requires and what justice demands.

Joséphine Karlsson is a defence lawyer of formidable ability and equally
formidable moral flexibility, whose career arc across the series has taken
her from straightforward criminal defence work into increasingly compromised
territory.

--- THE INVESTIGATION: THE YOUNG WOMAN'S DEATH ---

Series 8 opens with the discovery of the body of a young woman in Paris.
The circumstances of her death — where she was found, the condition of the
body, the evidence of what happened to her — establish the central investigation
that will run through the series.

The victim is connected to the world of sex work and exploitation in Paris —
a world that the show has visited in previous series and that it examines with
its characteristic refusal to simplify: the people in this world are not simply
victims or criminals but people in complex situations shaped by poverty,
immigration status, addiction, and the specific dynamics of exploitation.

The investigation identifies a network of individuals responsible for the
exploitation of vulnerable women in Paris — a network with connections to
legitimate business and to organised crime, operating through the specific
mechanisms of intimidation and dependency that such networks use.

--- GILOU'S SITUATION ---

Series 8 brings to a head the accumulated weight of Gilou's past conduct.
He is facing serious legal jeopardy — the consequences of actions in previous
series, specifically his handling of informants and the specific deaths or
harms connected to his operations, have not gone away. The institutional
machinery is moving toward him, and the question of whether he will face
prosecution, whether Laure will protect him or be unable to, and what the
cost of either outcome will be is one of the series' primary dramatic threads.

Gilou's situation forces Laure into the specific position she has been in
throughout the series: the choice between the job and the person, between
the institution and her loyalty to someone she loves.

--- JUDGE ROBAN'S ILLNESS ---

Judge Roban is seriously ill in Series 8. His physical decline runs parallel
to his continued engagement with the case he is investigating — a corruption
case with political dimensions that he has been pursuing with characteristic
dogged integrity. The illness frames his Series 8 arc as a kind of valediction:
a man who has spent his career trying to make the French legal system work as
it should, now facing the end of his ability to do that work.

His specific investigation into political corruption — the thread that connects
his judicial work to the broader institutional critique that runs through all
of Spiral — is brought to a conclusion in Series 8 that is true to the show's
consistent position: the system is corrupt, individuals of integrity can cause
damage to that corruption, but they cannot eliminate it.

--- JOSÉPHINE KARLSSON ---

Joséphine's arc in Series 8 involves her navigation of the consequences of
her own past compromises. She is a woman who has consistently chosen her
own survival and advancement over ethical purity, and who has paid costs for
those choices while also benefiting from them. Series 8 requires her to make
further choices in this vein, and the specific decisions she makes — and what
they cost her — bring her arc to a conclusion that is consistent with the
series' treatment of her throughout: intelligent, compromised, and not entirely
without redeeming quality.

--- THE INVESTIGATION'S DEVELOPMENT ---

The investigation of the young woman's murder develops through the series in
the standard Spiral fashion: leads pursued and found false, suspects identified
and cleared, the specific machinery of the French judicial system deployed
alongside the more direct methods of Berthaud's team.

The network responsible for the exploitation and ultimately the death of the
victim is gradually mapped: its specific members, their roles, the specific
financial and criminal structures through which it operates, and the connections
to legitimate Paris that protect it from straightforward prosecution.

The investigation intersects with Gilou's situation: the same criminal world
that is the subject of the murder investigation has connections to the
informant networks Gilou has been managing, and the specific individuals whose
testimony or cooperation Laure needs may also be people whose knowledge of
Gilou's past conduct gives them leverage over the investigation.

--- THE RESOLUTION OF THE INVESTIGATION ---

The murder investigation reaches its conclusion through the accumulated work
of Berthaud's team and Judge Roban's judicial process. The network is dismantled
sufficiently to bring the immediate perpetrators of the victim's death to
account, though the show is characteristically honest about what "dismantled"
means: some people are prosecuted, some escape, the underlying conditions
that produced the network are not changed by the investigation's success.

--- GILOU AND LAURE: THE SERIES FINALE ---

The series finale addresses the long arc of Laure and Gilou's relationship and
Gilou's legal jeopardy. The specific resolution — whether Gilou faces
prosecution, whether he and Laure reach a stable position, what each of them
has to give up or accept — is the emotional culmination of eight series of
sustained characterisation.

The show ends in the register it has maintained throughout: not triumphant,
not entirely bleak, but honest about the costs of everything — of the work,
of the compromises, of the specific loyalties that both sustain people and
destroy them.

--- NOTES ON THE SERIES ---

Series 8 is a fitting conclusion to one of the finest European crime dramas.
Its refusal to offer comfort — about institutions, about individuals, about
the possibility of justice in a system designed as much to protect power as
to serve it — is consistent from the first episode to the last. The central
characters end the show changed by what they have been through in ways that
are psychologically credible rather than dramatically convenient. Spiral's
achievement, across its eight series, is to have made procedural crime drama
genuinely serious without losing the forward propulsion that makes it watchable.

--- BACKGROUND AND CONTEXT ---

"Written in Blood" is an episode from Series 2 of ITV's Midsomer Murders,
first broadcast in 1999. It stars John Nettles as DCI Tom Barnaby and Daniel
Casey as DS Gavin Troy. The episode is based on Caroline Graham's novel of
the same name — one of the original Barnaby novels on which the television
series was based — and is set in the specific world of an amateur creative
writing group in the village of Fletcher's Cross.

The creative writing circle setting is one of the more characteristically
Midsomer settings: a small, closed community of people whose specific shared
activity provides both the social glue and the specific tensions that drive
the episode's events. Amateur writing groups are places where people share
their creative work and their private lives in specific ways that create specific
vulnerabilities and specific opportunities for the kinds of grievances and
revelations that Midsomer Murders consistently converts into murder.

The episode is also notable for featuring a specific complication in the form
of a visiting professional author — someone from outside the amateur circle
whose presence disrupts its specific internal dynamics in ways that prove fatal.

--- NOTES ON THE EPISODE ---

"Written in Blood" is one of the most characteristic early Midsomer Murders
episodes — the specific setting of the creative writing circle is exploited
with real ingenuity, and the relationship between the fictional work and the
actual events is one of the form's more satisfying specific devices. The episode
demonstrates the specific pleasure of the Midsomer format at its best: a closed
community with a specific shared activity, a cast of suspects each of whom has
something to hide, and a killer whose specific guilt is connected to the specific
world the episode has constructed.

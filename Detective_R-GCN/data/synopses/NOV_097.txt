Calamity Town transports Ellery Queen to idyllic Wrightsville, where he witnesses the return of black sheep Jim Haight to marry Nora Wright, only for arsenic poisoning to kill Jim's sister Rosemary, framing Jim amid small-town hysteria until Ellery exposes Nora's secret pregnancy and Rosemary's self-poisoning suicide to shield her sister's shame.
Wrightsville Idyll and Calamity House

Ellery Queen (incognito as "Ellery Smith") rents Calamity House—the unluckiest dwelling in Wrightsville, New England—from banker John Wright for writing seclusion. Built as wedding gift for daughter Nora, jinxed when fiancé Jim Haight vanished pre-ceremony three years prior, prior tenant dying mysteriously. Ellery befriends Wright daughters: vivacious Pat (romantic spark), fragile Nora (recluse post-jilting), scandalous Lola (disowned).

Jim returns, contrite alcoholic/gambler; despite family doubts, Nora insists wedding proceeds in Calamity House; Ellery relocates to Wrights', flirting with Pat amid Carter Setwick's jealousy.
Poison Letters and Nora's Illness

Ellery/Pat discover Jim's hidden cache: three dated letters (Thanksgiving, Christmas, New Year's) outlining Nora's arsenic poisoning/death—pre-written "confession" damning Jim. Nora sickens precisely on first date—arsenic confirmed; Ellery warns Jim, enlists doc Horstmann monitoring; town whispers murder plot.

New Year's Eve party at Calamity House: Rosemary Haight (Jim's sharp-tongued sister) drinks fatal poisoned cocktail meant for Nora; Jim arrested amid mob fury, trial looms.
Trial Hysteria and Family Secrets

Wrightsville erupts—first murder in decades sparks gossip, boycotts, lynch mobs; Jim's defense crumbles under letters, Nora's testimony, Rosemary's enmity. Ellery, sidelined, probes privately: Jim's innocence evident, letters' timing too perfect; Nora's frailty hides pregnancy (Jim's child, pre-jilting).

Rosemary learned Nora's secret, threatened exposure/blackmail; self-poisoned cocktail to frame Jim, sacrificing self for sister's "salvation" from scandal.
Ellery's Revelation and Town Reckoning

Post-trial, Ellery confronts Nora: letters Jim's drunken forgery at Rosemary's behest; she orchestrated poisoning attempt, suicide to seal frame—mercy killing her own future via disgrace. Nora confesses, takes own life via remaining arsenic; Jim freed, broken; Ellery departs Wrightsville scarred by small-town "calamity."

--- BACKGROUND AND CONTEXT ---

"Unforgotten" Series 2 was broadcast on ITV in October–November 2017. It
consists of six episodes and follows the series' established format: a cold
case investigation by DCI Cassie Stuart (Nicola Walker) and DS Sunny Khan
(Sanjeev Bhaskar), with the investigation of a decades-old death revealing its
connections to four present-day suspects whose lives are permanently altered
by the investigation.

Series 2 is distinguished from Series 1 by a shift in the nature of the original
crime: where Series 1's Jimmy Sullivan was an adult male victim whose death
involved a relatively conventional criminal act (however long suppressed), Series
2 involves the murder of a young man in the late 1990s whose death is connected
to a specific subculture and whose specific victimhood involves the particular
vulnerabilities of gay men in a period when those vulnerabilities were still
acute. The series uses this context with the same care and specificity that
Unforgotten consistently brings to its social and historical settings.

--- THE COLD CASE: DAVID WALKER ---

Human remains are discovered — a skeleton found in the foundations of a building
under demolition, concealed there for approximately twenty years. The skeleton
is identified as David Walker, a young gay man who disappeared in the late 1990s.
His disappearance was reported at the time but was not adequately investigated —
the investigation of missing young men, particularly those from marginalised
communities, in the late 1990s did not always receive the resources and attention
that subsequent standards would require.

David was, the investigation establishes, a young man who inhabited the specific
world of late-1990s gay London in ways that created both connection and specific
vulnerability: the clubs, the social networks, the particular dynamics of a
community that was still navigating the aftermath of the AIDS crisis while
simultaneously experiencing a specific cultural flourishing.

--- THE FOUR SUSPECTS ---

The investigation identifies four people whose lives in the late 1990s connected
them to David Walker and to the circumstances of his death:

Marion Kelsey (Rosie Cavaliero) — a woman who is now a prison officer, a mother,
a person whose present life is entirely ordered around service and responsibility.
Her specific connection to David Walker and to the events of the late 1990s
is buried in a past she has not visited in decades.

Colin Osborne (Mark Bonnar) — a man who has built a successful business and
a settled family life, whose specific history with David Walker is part of
the investigation's reconstruction. His specific relationship to the events
involves both personal history and specific acts whose accountability the
investigation must establish.

Elise Penning (Hannah Gordon) — an older woman, a figure of established
community reputation, whose specific connection to the case is among the
more surprising of the four and whose specific history with the period and
its dynamics is the investigation's most carefully held revelation.

Tony Wetherall (Peter Egan) — a retired professional man whose specific
position in the late-1990s world connected to David Walker creates a specific
set of questions about what he knew, what he did, and what he has been
managing since.

--- THE LATE-1990S WORLD ---

The reconstruction of David Walker's life and the events of his death requires
understanding the specific world he inhabited: the gay scene in London in the
late 1990s, with its specific social dynamics, its specific vulnerabilities,
and its specific cultural character.

The investigation's reconstruction of this world — through the accounts of
the suspects, through surviving contemporaries, and through the physical evidence
of the original investigation — is handled with the specificity and the respect
that Unforgotten brings to all its historical contexts. The series is attentive
to the specific conditions of gay life in the period: the specific forms of
marginalisation that were still structural in British society in the late 1990s,
the specific community resilience that existed alongside that marginalisation,
and the specific vulnerabilities that produced in gay men of the period a
relationship to institutional protection that was different from the majority.

--- CASSIE AND SUNNY'S INVESTIGATION ---

The investigation proceeds with the specific procedural thoroughness that defines
the series. Cassie and Sunny's approach — patient, methodical, attentive to
both the physical evidence and the human picture — assembles the case across
six episodes with the accumulated weight of a cold case whose specific difficulty
is the gap between a late-1990s world and the present day: witnesses have moved,
evidence has degraded, and the specific community that knew David Walker has
dispersed.

Cassie's specific emotional investment in the case — her care about David Walker
as a person, not simply as a victim — is one of the series' most consistent
elements. The investigations she conducts are never simply procedural: she is
always interested in who the victim was, and the specific humanity she brings
to that interest is what distinguishes her from more conventionally detached
investigators.

--- THE PRESSURE ON THE PRESENT LIVES ---

Series 2's four suspects, like Series 1's, have built lives in the decades
since the original crime. The specific ways in which the investigation disrupts
those lives — the specific people who learn things they had not known, the
specific relationships whose foundations are shaken by what the investigation
reveals — are handled with Unforgotten's characteristic attention to the human
cost of truth.

The specific impact on the suspects' families — spouses who are learning for
the first time the dimensions of their partners' histories, children whose
understanding of their parents is being permanently altered — is given
meaningful space. The show is not indifferent to the cost of investigation.
It insists on its necessity while acknowledging its cost.

--- THE TRUTH OF DAVID WALKER'S DEATH ---

The investigation assembles the full picture of what happened to David Walker.
The truth involves a specific gathering in the late 1990s at which David was
present, during which an act of homophobic violence occurred that led directly
to his death. The person who struck the fatal blow was Ray Dawson — but the
distribution of responsibility extends across the group who were present.
Colin Osborne was directly complicit in what happened and in the subsequent
concealment; Marion Kelsey and others present did not intervene and participated
in the cover-up to varying degrees over the following two decades.

The specific distribution of responsibility among the four suspects — those
who acted directly, those who were present and did not intervene, those who
helped manage the aftermath, and those who have been carrying knowledge of
varying degrees of directness for twenty years — is the investigation's most
morally complex achievement.

--- RESOLUTION ---

The full picture of David Walker's death is established and the appropriate
legal process is engaged. The specific people responsible — to whatever degree
they are responsible — face the specific consequences of the truth's emergence.

The series ends with the characteristic Unforgotten quality: the truth has been
found, a form of justice has been done, and the cost of both — to the suspects,
their families, and the investigative team — is acknowledged. Cassie and Sunny
return to the work.

--- NOTES ON THE SERIES ---

Unforgotten Series 2 maintains and in some ways deepens the qualities of the
first series. The late-1990s gay London setting is handled with real specificity
and genuine respect — the show does not treat the period's specific conditions
as simply backdrop but as a world whose specific character shaped the specific
people in the case. The four-suspect ensemble, with its careful distribution
of guilt and complicity, gives the investigation its characteristic moral
complexity. And Nicola Walker and Sanjeev Bhaskar continue to be one of British
crime drama's most quietly excellent detective partnerships.

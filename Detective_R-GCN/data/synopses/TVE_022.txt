TVE_022 – Columbo: Columbo Goes to College

In this episode, Columbo investigates the murder of a college student, Paula Goodman, who is found dead in a manner that is meant to look like a suicide or a tragic accident. The case is set in a small liberal‑arts college where the atmosphere is idealistic and boosterish, and the faculty is eager to protect the institution’s reputation.

The prime suspect immediately is a student radical, who is known to oppose the university’s leadership and has a history of protests. The police lean toward the idea that the radical killed Paula in a fit of rage, then tried to make it look like an accident. But Columbo notices that the movement and the placement of the body do not fit a chaotic, emotionally charged crime. The scene is too controlled.

The real killer is a tenured professor, whose identity is suppressed in the episode until the final act. The professor has a weakness: he is dependent on a powerful dean who has protected him from past scandals in exchange for favors. The dean has recently decided to fire the professor for academic misconduct, and the professor retaliates by killing the dean in a way that makes the crime appear to be the work of the student radical.

The setup:

    The dean is invited to a closed campus event,

    The professor tampers with a piece of equipment or a door mechanism,

    The apparatus then crushes the dean in a locked‑room‑style accident,

    The radical student is framed by planting his jacket at the scene and arranging for witnesses to see him near the building. =

Columbo’s first clue is the neatness of the evidence. The student’s jacket fits the scene too perfectly. The way it’s placed, the way the buttons are undone, and the lack of sweat or struggle make it look staged. The radical, when questioned, is genuinely confused and has no motive beyond general anger at the administration.

Columbo then turns to the timeline of the event. The accident is timed to occur during a lecture that the professor is known to be giving. Witnesses remember hearing the professor’s voice at the time of the accident, but Columbo discovers that the professor could have been in the dean’s office at the same moment, using an old campus passageway or a side door that is not on the main security logs.

The final clue is the door mechanism. The accident is made to look like a mechanical failure, but the mechanism is highly specific and requires a manual adjustment. The type of adjustment required matches the professor’s known expertise: he has written papers on campus architecture and engineering. The dean’s office door is one of the few places in the building where that exact mechanism is used.

Columbo confronts the professor by re‑enacting the sequence of the accident, pointing out that the only person with the combination of motive, access, and technical knowledge is the professor. The professor, realizing he’s been caught, admits to the murder. The episode ends with the college’s idealistic image still intact, but the truth quietly buried: the radical is exonerated, and the tenure‑track killer is arrested.

S-Town begins as Brian Reed investigates horologist John B. McLemore's claim of a Woodstock, Alabama murder cover-up by scion Kabrahm White, but pivots to John's suicide, explosive inheritance feuds over rumored gold, and revelations of his closeted bisexuality, mercury poisoning, and doomsday worldview amid small-town tensions. The seven-chapter arc shifts from true crime to intimate portrait of John's tormented life—ending with estate battles, Tyler's taser death, and Brian's reflections on consent, exploitation, and Shittown's raw humanity.
Chapter I: Murder Claim and Woodstock Arrival

John B. McLemore, brilliant but suicidal clock restorer despising his "Shittown" (Woodstock, Alabama), emails This American Life's Brian Reed to expose Kabrahm White's alleged murder of Dylan Nichols, bragged about to Jake Goodson. Brian visits John's cluttered horology paradise amid rural decay; Tyler Goodson, John's tattooed assistant, bonds over clockwork and climate despair; Skylar (Jake's wife) corroborates the boast, but Kabrahm denies, claiming Dylan died racing dirt bikes.​
Chapter II: Murder Fizzle and John's Despair

Police dismiss cover-up—no Dylan murder file; John rages at injustice, tattoos Tyler's neck "memento mori," rants on climate collapse, mercury poisoning (hat-making mercury nitrate), and repressed homosexuality in Bible Belt stagnation. John's mother Mary Grace, fading, shares clock tales; Tyler reveals John's clock tower obsession, hidden gold rumors; Brian senses John's deeper plea for exposure beyond crime.​
Chapter III: John's Suicide and Estate Feud

John arsenic-suinsides days after Brian leaves, citing mercury madness and futility; funeral draws Tyler vs. cousin Reta (John's caretaker); Tyler claims John's verbal bequest—house, possessions, alleged gold stash—Reta contests via lawyer, alleging Tyler's gold-nipple-ring theft and falling-out. Brian returns amid threats; Tyler vows court or vigilante justice over assets.​
Chapter IV: Tyler's Troubles and Town Secrets

Tyler's life unravels—tattoo empire crumbling, wife Kayla leaving, Reta's family circling estate; John's manifesto surfaces, railing against Shittown's meth, racism, homophobia; Tyler admits gay encounters, John's unrequited love, their "bromance" blurring lines. Gold hunt yields nothing; Reta reveals John's bisexuality, estate worth little.​
Chapter V: Inheritance Battle and Revelations

Court favors Reta; Tyler spirals—fights, theft accusations, Kayla's pregnancy by another; John's sexuality confirmed via lifelong crushes, repressed by faith; mercury confirmed via hair samples, exacerbating paranoia. Brian grapples ethics—John's consent for exposure? Town's judgment? Tyler's volatility hints tragedy.​
Chapter VI: Tyler's Death and Aftermath

Tyler dies tasered by police during arrest for threats; Kayla births daughter; Reta sells house piecemeal; Brian visits clock tower, reflects on John's genius-isolation, consent boundaries in storytelling.​
Chapter VII: John's Legacy and Shittown Truth

Final tapes: John's eloquence on time, environment, sexuality, suicide rationale—mercury, unrequited love, climate despair. Brian honors John's wish for raw portrait, exposing Shittown's beauty/brutality; no murder, but lives' quiet desperations laid bare.

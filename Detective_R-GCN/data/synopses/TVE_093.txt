--- BACKGROUND AND CONTEXT ---

"Spiral" (French: Engrenages) Series 4 was broadcast on Canal+ in France in
2012 and reached international audiences through BBC Four in 2012–2013. It
consists of twelve episodes — the longest series to that point — and continues
the show's sustained engagement with the Paris criminal justice system through
a central murder investigation and the ongoing institutional and personal arcs
of its main characters.

Series 4 is notable for several reasons: it deepens the consequences of Series
3's events significantly, particularly for Gilou and for Laure; it introduces
new characters who will be significant to the series going forward; and it
develops the political corruption thread that Judge Roban has been pursuing
into territory that puts him in genuine institutional danger. The central case
is one of the series' most complex, involving organised crime at the intersection
of the Paris criminal world and the legitimate business community.

--- THE CENTRAL CASE: THE CIRCUIT BREAKER ---

Series 4's central investigation begins with a death that occurs in circumstances
suggesting organised criminal involvement — a figure in the Paris underworld
whose death initiates an investigation into the specific network of criminal
operations that surrounds it.

The investigation opens into a specific and complex criminal ecology: a network
of individuals and organizations whose activities span legitimate business
and organised crime, whose connections reach into the French financial and
business world, and whose specific operations include elements of money
laundering, violence, and the exploitation of the specific opportunities that
the intersection of legal and illegal provides.

Laure Berthaud's team — reconstituted after the events of Series 3 and operating
under the specific institutional pressures that the previous series generated —
investigates this network with the procedural thoroughness that defines the
show's approach. The specific mechanisms of the network's operation, its
hierarchical structure, and the specific individuals at its various levels
are established through the investigation's development.

--- THE CONSEQUENCES OF SERIES 3: GILOU ---

Series 4 opens with Gilou Escoffier in a position of significant legal jeopardy
as a consequence of events in Series 3. The specific charges against him —
rooted in his management of informants and the specific conduct that the previous
investigation brought to light — are being processed through the institutional
machinery. He is under investigation. He is at risk of prosecution. And the
specific impact of his situation on his working relationship with Laure —
and on her own professional standing, which is complicated by her long knowledge
of his conduct — is one of the series' primary dramatic threads from the outset.

The question of what happens to Gilou — whether he is prosecuted, whether the
case against him holds, and what Laure does in relation to his situation —
is never simple in the Spiral framework. Gilou is not simply a corrupt officer
who deserves prosecution: he is a specific person with a specific history whose
specific conduct was shaped by the same pressures that make policing in Paris's
criminal world as complicated as the show has consistently shown it to be.

Laure's navigation of her obligations — to the institution, to the investigation,
to Gilou personally — is one of Series 4's most sustained and most difficult
threads.

--- LAURE'S PERSONAL SITUATION ---

Series 4 also develops Laure's personal situation in ways that the series has
been building toward: her relationship with Gilou, which has been the show's
central personal relationship across its run, reaches a specific point of
development in Series 4. The professional complications of their relationship —
the specific ways in which their personal connection creates professional
problems and vice versa — are brought to a head by the institutional pressure
that Gilou's situation generates.

Laure is also navigating her own institutional standing. Her specific methods —
the willingness to push beyond official procedure, the management of evidence
in ways that are not always strictly sanctioned — have accumulated a history
that the institutional machinery can use against her if it chooses. Series 4
makes clear that the machinery is aware of this history and that its deployment
is not simply a matter of professional consequence but of institutional politics.

--- JUDGE ROBAN'S CORRUPTION INVESTIGATION ---

Roban's corruption investigation — the thread he has been pursuing across
multiple series — reaches its most dangerous phase in Series 4. He has been
pursuing connections between organised crime and the French political and
business world, and the specific figures he has now identified are powerful
enough that their protection of themselves constitutes a genuine institutional
threat to his investigation and to him personally.

The specific mechanisms through which political and financial power in France
can suppress a judicial investigation — the appointments, the transfers, the
specific ways that institutional pressure can be applied to a judge pursuing
cases that inconvenience powerful people — are rendered with Spiral's
characteristic specificity. Roban is not simply facing professional pressure:
he is operating in a context where the people he is investigating have the
specific capability to end his judicial career or worse.

His specific response to this threat — the choices he makes about how to pursue
the investigation, what he discloses and what he protects, and how he manages
his own safety within the institutional framework — is one of Series 4's most
compelling threads.

--- JOSÉPHINE KARLSSON: A DIFFERENT DIRECTION ---

Joséphine's Series 4 arc takes her in a somewhat different direction from the
previous series. Having reached the limit of certain forms of professional
compromise in Series 3, she faces the specific consequences of her choices
and must navigate a professional landscape that is more constrained than it was.

Her specific trajectory in Series 4 involves a case that connects to the central
investigation in ways that create both professional and personal complications.
The specific nature of her involvement — whether as defence lawyer, as someone
with knowledge of the case's relevant world, or in some more directly implicated
capacity — develops as the series progresses.

Joséphine's evolution across the series — from the aggressive but technically
ethical defence lawyer of Series 1 toward the more compromised figure of
subsequent series — reaches a specific inflection point in Series 4 that is
consistent with the show's overall treatment of her: she is not simply falling
but is making specific choices that are understandable given her specific
history and circumstances, even when those choices are clearly wrong.

--- THE INVESTIGATION'S DEVELOPMENT ---

The central investigation of Series 4 proceeds through its twelve episodes
with the sustained procedural attention that defines the Spiral format. The
specific work — following the money, tracing the connections between the criminal
network and its legitimate business cover, identifying the specific individuals
whose conduct is criminal within the broader network — is rendered with the
accuracy and detail that has been the show's consistent standard.

Key developments include:

The mapping of the criminal network's specific structure: its hierarchy, its
membership, and the specific mechanisms through which it operates and protects
itself.

The identification of specific victims connected to the network — people whose
deaths or disappearances are part of its operation and whose fates are part
of what the investigation must establish.

The specific intersection between the criminal network and Roban's corruption
investigation: the connections between organised crime at the level Laure's
team is investigating and the political and business world at the level Roban
is pursuing are gradually established.

The management of the specific legal and procedural complications that arise
as the investigation develops: the PACE equivalent frameworks of French law,
the examining magistrate process, and the specific ways in which evidence must
be gathered and managed to survive legal challenge.

--- THE RESOLUTION ---

The central criminal case of Series 4 reaches its resolution through the
combined work of Laure's investigation and Roban's judicial process. The
specific perpetrators of the case's central crimes are identified and brought
to account through the institutional mechanisms — arrest, charge, prosecution —
that the French system provides.

The resolution is, characteristically, partial: the immediate criminal actors
are addressed while the broader network — its political protection, its
institutional connections — is addressed only in part. Series 4 advances the
institutional accountability significantly but does not conclude it: the
corruption investigation that Roban has been pursuing is at a stage of
development at the series' conclusion that represents genuine progress while
leaving the larger question of accountability unresolved.

--- GILOU'S FATE ---

The specific resolution of Gilou's legal situation in Series 4 — whether he
is prosecuted, what the outcome of the investigation against him is, and what
this means for his career and his relationship with Laure — is one of the
series' most significant conclusions. Spiral does not provide a simple answer:
the institutional machinery processes Gilou's case through the mechanisms
available to it, and the outcome is both specific and consistent with the
show's understanding of how institutional processes work.

What happens to Gilou — and what Laure does in relation to whatever happens —
is the series' most personal conclusion, and it is handled with the emotional
honesty that the show has brought to the Laure-Gilou relationship throughout
its run.

--- NOTES ON THE SERIES ---

Series 4 of Spiral is one of the most ambitious seasons in the show's run —
the longest in episode count, the most complex in its layering of investigative,
institutional, and personal threads. Its specific achievement is the sustained
development of the consequences of previous series: the show's world has been
built across multiple series, and Series 4 exploits that accumulated history
with considerable sophistication. The Gilou situation — and its impact on Laure —
represents the series' most direct engagement with the question of what loyalty
costs within a system that demands integrity, and the answer it provides is
honest and uncomfortable.

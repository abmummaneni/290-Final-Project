POD_030 | Over My Dead Body: Tally | Wondery | Podcast
================================================================================
FULL PODCAST SYNOPSIS — SPOILERS INCLUDED
================================================================================

--- BACKGROUND AND CONTEXT ---

"Over My Dead Body: Tally" is a podcast produced by Wondery, hosted by Matthew
Shaer, released in 2019. "Tally" is the first season of the "Over My Dead Body"
series, which covers different cases in subsequent seasons. It tells the story
of the murder of Dan Markel, a Florida State University law professor, in
Tallahassee in 2014 — one of the most unusual and disturbing murder-for-hire
cases in recent American legal history, involving a prominent academic, a bitter
post-divorce family dispute, and the involvement of a Cuban-American organized
crime family in South Florida.

The podcast is particularly well-regarded for the quality of its narrative
construction and for the genuine complexity of the case — a web of relationships,
motivations, and criminal connections that the podcast patiently untangles
across its episodes.

--- DAN MARKEL ---

Daniel Markel was a forty-one-year-old professor of criminal law at Florida
State University's College of Law. He was Canadian-born, Harvard-educated,
well-regarded in his field, and by multiple accounts a devoted and loving
father to his two young sons. He was divorced from Wendi Adelson, and their
divorce — while legally resolved — had left a difficult co-parenting situation
and a relationship with Wendi's family that was far from warm.

On July 18, 2014, Markel was shot twice in the head in the garage of his
Tallahassee home as he returned from the gym. He died in hospital the following
day. The shooting was captured on his neighbor's security camera, showing
the killer's vehicle.

--- THE INVESTIGATION: TALLAHASSEE POLICE AND THE FBI ---

The Tallahassee Police Department and eventually the FBI investigated the
murder. The investigation was immediately puzzling: Markel had no enemies in
any conventional sense. He was a law professor, a legal blogger, a community
figure. Nobody could identify an obvious motive from his professional or
personal life in Tallahassee.

The investigation focused on the surveillance footage — a vehicle that had
followed Markel, two men in it, a methodical ambush at his garage. This was
not a random crime. It was planned and executed by people who had researched
Markel's routine. The professional quality of the surveillance and the shooting
pointed toward paid killers.

--- THE ADELSON FAMILY ---

As the investigation expanded, it began to look toward Wendi Adelson's family —
specifically her brother Charlie Adelson, a successful periodontist in Miami,
and her mother Donna Adelson. The Adelsons had a complicated and not affectionate
relationship with Dan Markel. The specific tension centered on a custody dispute:
the Adelsons wanted Wendi and the children to move to South Florida, closer
to the family. Markel objected and fought the proposed relocation through the
courts. His opposition to the move was a source of significant anger within
the Adelson family.

The podcast establishes the Adelson family dynamics in considerable detail:
the specific role of Charlie as the family's dominant male figure, the mother
Donna's intense involvement in her children's lives, and the specific quality
of their hostility toward Markel — a hostility that went beyond ordinary post-
divorce friction.

--- SIGFREDO GARCIA AND KATHERINE MAGBANUA ---

The investigation identified two men — Sigfredo Garcia and Luis Rivera — as
the actual shooters. Their connection to the case ran through a woman named
Katherine Magbanua, who was the linchpin of the entire conspiracy.

Katherine Magbanua had a specific connection to both sides of the case:

She was the former girlfriend of Sigfredo Garcia and the mother of his child.
This gave her access to Garcia and his criminal world.

She was also, at the time of the murder, in a romantic relationship with
Charlie Adelson — Dan Markel's brother-in-law. This connection to the
Adelson family is the bridge between the people who wanted Markel dead and
the people who killed him.

The prosecution's theory is that Charlie Adelson (and possibly his mother
Donna) contracted the murder through Katherine Magbanua, who in turn used
her connection to Sigfredo Garcia to hire the actual killers.

--- THE COMPLEX EVIDENCE ---

The case against the various parties was built from a combination of:

Financial records showing unexplained payments to Katherine Magbanua in the
period around the murder.

Cell phone data showing the movements of Garcia, Rivera, and Magbanua in
the period before and after the killing, placing them in Tallahassee at the
relevant times.

Witness testimony from people in the criminal world connected to Garcia
and Rivera.

Surveillance footage from the crime scene identifying the vehicle.

A key undercover FBI operation in which an agent posed as a criminal contact
and recorded conversations with people connected to the conspiracy.

--- THE TRIALS ---

Sigfredo Garcia and Luis Rivera were convicted of first-degree murder and
sentenced to life in prison.

Katherine Magbanua was tried twice. Her first trial, in 2019, ended in a hung
jury — closely divided. Her second trial, in 2021, resulted in conviction on
all counts, including first-degree murder. She was sentenced to life in prison.

The podcast covers the Garcia and Rivera prosecutions and the first Magbanua
trial in its main run, with updates on subsequent developments.

--- CHARLIE AND DONNA ADELSON ---

Charlie Adelson and his mother Donna Adelson have not been charged as of the
podcast's original release. The prosecution's case presented them as the
originators of the murder conspiracy — the people who wanted Markel dead
and who used Magbanua as the conduit to Garcia and Rivera — but bringing
criminal charges against them required a quality of direct evidence that,
through the period covered by the podcast, was not yet available.

This is the case's most frustrating open thread: the people who arguably
bear the greatest moral responsibility for Dan Markel's murder have not
been prosecuted, while the people who carried out the actual killing have
been convicted and imprisoned.

NOTE: After the podcast's original release, in 2023, Charlie Adelson was
arrested and charged with first-degree murder and other offenses related to
Dan Markel's killing. Donna Adelson was also charged. These developments
occurred after the podcast's initial run but are part of the case's continuing
resolution.

--- THE PODCAST'S NARRATIVE STRENGTHS ---

Matthew Shaer's narration is praised for its novelistic quality — the podcast
tells the story of the Markel case with unusual attention to the human
dimensions of all the people involved. The portrait of Dan Markel as a person
(drawn from interviews with colleagues, friends, and family) is particularly
affecting. The specific tragedy of a man who died because of a custody dispute
— because he insisted on his right to be present in his children's lives in
Tallahassee rather than allowing them to be moved to Miami — gives the case
a painful irony that the podcast does not avoid.

--- SUMMARY ---

"Over My Dead Body: Tally" is one of the finest podcast treatments of a complex
murder-for-hire case. Its narrative is rich, its investigation is thorough,
and its moral center — the murdered father, the sons who lost him, the slow
unraveling of a conspiracy that ran from a Miami dental practice to a
Tallahassee garage — is consistently maintained. The primary resolution (the
conviction of the actual killers and the intermediary) is achieved within the
podcast's run, and the subsequent charging of the Adelsons represents the
fuller justice that the case demanded.

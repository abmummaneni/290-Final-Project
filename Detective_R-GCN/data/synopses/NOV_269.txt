NOV_269 — Cause of Death
Patricia Cornwell, 1996
Full Plot Synopsis (Kay Scarpetta #7)

SERIES CONTEXT

Dr. Kay Scarpetta is the Chief Medical Examiner of Virginia, based in Richmond. She is a
forensic pathologist of exceptional skill and a recurring target of dangerous individuals
drawn to her high-profile investigations. Her niece Lucy Farinelli works in law enforcement
technology; her close friend Pete Marino is a Richmond detective; Benton Wesley is a
behavioral scientist with the FBI and Scarpetta's romantic partner.

THE BODY IN THE WATER


New Year's Eve Dive

On New Year's Eve, a body is found in the waters near a decommissioned nuclear power plant
called the Inactive Ship Facility in the Elizabeth River, Virginia. The dead man is Ted
Eddings, a journalist. He was found in his diving gear, apparently the victim of a diving
accident. But the circumstances raise immediate questions: what was he diving for, why here,
and why now?

Scarpetta is called in. She examines the body and almost immediately determines that the
death was not accidental. The wounds, the physical evidence, the scene — none of it is
consistent with a straightforward diving accident. Eddings was murdered.


What Eddings Was Investigating

Ted Eddings was a journalist working on a story involving a radical doomsday cult known as
the New Zionists. This group had apocalyptic beliefs and, more urgently, was pursuing
chemical and biological weapons. Eddings had been investigating their activities and was
close to something — close enough to be killed for it.

The decommissioned nuclear facility where he was diving was connected to the cult's
operations in ways that become clearer as Scarpetta investigates.

THE NEW ZIONISTS


Beliefs and Leadership

The New Zionists are a heavily armed, theologically extreme group led by a charismatic and
deeply dangerous man named Joel Hand. They believe in an imminent apocalypse and in
accelerating it — or surviving it — through the acquisition of weapons of mass destruction.
They are specifically focused on chemical weapons: nerve agents and variants capable of
mass casualties.

Hand is intelligent, messianic, and utterly without scruple. He has built the cult through
a combination of ideological fervor and calculated violence.


The Weapons Connection

The cult has been acquiring materials and technical knowledge to produce chemical agents.
Their connection to the old nuclear facility relates to access — to materials, to routes, to
a location that has gaps in monitoring and oversight. Eddings had been piecing together the
supply chain and was about to publish findings that would have exposed the operation and
brought federal attention.

His murder was directly ordered to prevent his story from running.

SCARPETTA'S INVESTIGATION


Forensic Detail

True to the Cornwell formula, the investigation is grounded in meticulous forensic pathology.
Scarpetta works through the physical evidence of Eddings's death, the chemical traces on
his equipment, the wound patterns, the timeline. The forensic work is technically precise
and forms the backbone of the investigation's credibility — Cornwell was embedded with the
Virginia medical examiner's office for research and the procedural detail shows.


Lucy's Role

Lucy Farinelli, Scarpetta's niece, is involved in the investigation through her technical
expertise. Lucy is brilliant with computers and technology and has been building her law
enforcement career, though her relationship with her aunt is complicated — Lucy is intense,
sometimes reckless, and carries wounds from a difficult childhood that manifest in her
professional relationships. In this novel she is also navigating her sexuality.

Her technical work helps track the cult's communications and activities.


Marino and Wesley

Pete Marino provides ground-level detective work and the irreplaceable function of saying
what no one else will say. His relationship with Scarpetta is one of the series' constants
— he respects her and is sometimes threatened by her; they bicker and depend on each other.
Benton Wesley, as Scarpetta's partner in both the professional and personal sense, brings
the FBI behavioral and intelligence angle while also representing Scarpetta's emotional life,
which is perpetually under pressure from the demands of her work.


Personal Stakes

As the investigation deepens and the New Zionists become aware that Scarpetta is closing in,
the danger to her and the people around her escalates. Cornwell's Scarpetta novels consistently
escalate from forensic puzzle to direct physical threat, and this novel follows that arc.
Scarpetta and those close to her are increasingly in the crosshairs of a group that has
already demonstrated its willingness to kill.

RESOLUTION

The investigation leads to a direct confrontation with the New Zionists and Joel Hand.
The threat they represent — the chemical weapons, the operational capability they have been
building — is neutralized through a combination of law enforcement action, FBI involvement,
and Scarpetta's refusal to be intimidated out of the investigation.

Hand is brought down. The chemical weapons operation is dismantled. Eddings's murder is
accounted for.

The personal cost of the investigation — to Scarpetta's relationships, to her sense of
safety, to the people around her — is real. The novel ends in Cornwell's characteristic
mode: the immediate threat resolved, the larger world still dangerous, Scarpetta still
standing and still working, her capacity for this life both her greatest strength and the
thing that most isolates her.

The Gallows Bird (original title Olycksfågeln) weaves two main cases together in Tanumshede/Fjällbacka: a string of supposed drunk‑driving “accidents” that are actually murders, and the killing of a reality‑show contestant. Patrik Hedström gradually uncovers that the accident victims form a long‑running pattern tied to a single killer with roots in a wealthy local family, and that the TV murder is a separate, more opportunistic crime.
Opening: Car Crash and Reality Show Arrive

A woman named Marit Kaspersen has a vicious argument with her partner, storms out of the house and drives off in a rage. Soon after, her car slams into a tree and she is found dead behind the wheel; the car reeks of alcohol, and her blood alcohol level is extremely high. On the surface it looks like a classic drunk‑driving tragedy by an unstable woman after a domestic fight.

Patrik Hedström from the Tanumshede police is called to the scene and is immediately bothered by small details that do not sit right with him. Marit was not known as a drinker, and people close to her insist she “hardly drank a drop,” which makes her sky‑high alcohol reading suspicious rather than explanatory.

At the same time, a coachload of reality‑TV participants arrives in town to film a new series, “Tanum Entertains” (or similar), in which minor celebrities from other shows live and work locally while cameras follow their every move. The local dignitaries hope the show will bring good publicity and tourism; the townspeople, and especially Patrik and his colleagues, mostly dread the disruption and circus atmosphere. The production quickly becomes chaotic: the contestants are fractious, they drink heavily, fight, flirt and stir up resentments both among themselves and with the locals.
Domestic Background: Erica, Patrik, and Anna

Away from the cases, Patrik’s private life is busy and stressed. He and Erica Falck, now living together with their baby, are in full wedding‑planning mode and constantly juggling childcare, work and family expectations.

Erica’s sister Anna, who suffered brutal domestic abuse in earlier books and ultimately killed her husband in self‑defence, is living with Erica while trying to rebuild her life. Her presence—damaged, fragile, protective of her young children—adds emotional weight and background tension in the house, as Erica tries to support her while also preparing for a supposedly happy new chapter.

These domestic threads run throughout the book: Patrik’s fatigue and divided attention, Erica’s exhaustion and family conflicts, and Anna’s slow psychological recovery all shadow Patrik’s ability to focus fully on what initially seems like a minor traffic fatality.
The “Accident” Becomes Suspicious

Back at the station, Patrik refuses to treat Marit’s death as an open‑and‑shut drunk‑driving accident. He orders more detailed forensic work and begins re‑interviewing people in her life, especially:

    Her partner, devastated and guilty about their argument.

    Her daughter, Sophie, who is particularly close to Marit’s female lover and deeply shaken by the loss.

Interviews and background checks confirm that Marit was not a habitual drinker and in fact rarely drank at all. This stark contradiction—heavy alcohol in her system versus her real habits—pushes Patrik toward the idea that the alcohol test reading might be the key to something staged, not an explanation of careless behavior.

As he digs deeper, Patrik uncovers similar past cases elsewhere in Sweden: single‑car “accidents” involving female victims who supposedly drove drunk into trees or off the road. Each victim had been portrayed as irresponsible and alcohol‑abusing, but relatives and friends in several cases insisted the women did not drink heavily. A pattern begins to emerge: scattered across the country, but similar victim profiles and similar crashes.
The Reality Show Murder

While the puzzle of Marit’s death grows more intriguing, the TV production explodes into scandal. The participants, already quarrelsome, throw an alcohol‑soaked party that spirals out of control: arguments, flirtations, petty jealousies and rivalries flare in front of the cameras and off them.

During or soon after this party, one of the contestants—an unpopular, abrasive figure who has made enemies among the others—is found dead. The body is discovered in a setting that clearly indicates murder rather than accident: injuries inconsistent with a fall or drunken mishap, and evidence that the victim was attacked.

With the cameras having shadowed the cast, there is extensive footage of tensions, threats and possible motives among the contestants and crew. The police are forced to work under intense media scrutiny:

    Producers resist anything that might halt filming or damage ratings.

    The public is fascinated, watching what has become a “real” murder mystery in real time.

    The participants themselves are both witnesses and suspects, and they keep performing for the cameras, complicating their statements.

Initially, Patrik has to juggle what look like two separate investigations: a maybe‑not‑accidental crash of a local woman, and a very deliberate murder within the reality‑show cast.
Linking the Crash to Older “Accidents”

Patrik insists on following his hunch about Marit’s case, despite pressure and the distraction of the TV murder. He sends queries to other police districts and digs into archives, discovering a series of female “drunk driving” deaths that have small but telling similarities to Marit’s crash.

As more data arrives, he starts to see that these accidents:

    Involve women with little or no history of heavy drinking.

    Show strikingly similar accident profiles: single‑car collisions or crashes into trees or obstacles.

    Feature blood tests with unexpectedly high alcohol levels.

These patterns strongly suggest a serial killer who stages deaths as drunk‑driving accidents, likely by intoxicating the victims and arranging the crash. The killer’s advantage is that most of these deaths were quickly written off by local authorities, with minimal investigation once alcohol was detected.

From there, Patrik and his colleagues begin reconstructing the victims’ backgrounds to look for a common link between them. This involves interviews with families, revisiting old scenes, and tracing any shared acquaintance or institution that might connect women from different regions of Sweden.
The Serial Killer’s Profile Emerges

As the team overlays the different cases, a clearer picture forms of the killer’s method and possible motives. He:

    Targets women who can plausibly be painted as having emotional or moral “weaknesses” (infidelity, relationship crises, marginalization).

    Ensures their bodies show high alcohol levels, making it easy to blame them.

    Stages the car crash in a way that appears consistent with a drunk losing control.

One of the crucial steps in the investigation is recognizing a specific, recurring connection between the victims: a link to a certain place or person in Tanumshede that ties back many years. Patrik realizes that what looked like scattered, unrelated tragedies are in fact the work of a single person who has moved or operated in multiple towns but is anchored in the area around Tanumshede.

This realisation turns the “local woman’s accident” into the tip of a much older and broader serial‑murder pattern, now finally reaching Patrik’s jurisdiction.
Parallel Tracks: TV Case vs. Accident Case

On the TV‑show side, the investigation moves along more “standard” lines: motive, opportunity, interpersonal conflict. The dead contestant had clashed with several others and with production staff. Suspicious elements include:

    On‑camera arguments and insults.

    Private entanglements (jealousy, affairs, grudges) among contestants.

    Possible financial or publicity motives, as some people stand to gain attention or sympathy from the tragedy.

The show’s producers try to keep filming and spin the crime into higher ratings—exactly what happens, as the viewership spikes to watch the murder investigation unfold. The police have to fight through deliberate obstruction by the production team, who fear shutdown and liability, as well as manage the pressure of constant media coverage.

While both cases demand time, Patrik more and more suspects that the Marit/accident investigation is the key to something “larger” and more dangerous than the messy but more contained TV murder.
The Wealthy Family and Old Secrets

As Patrik’s team maps out connections between the crash victims, the trail leads back toward a well‑off local family with a respectable public face and deep roots in the area. Years‑old tragedies and social dynamics begin to surface:

    Old relationships and betrayals.

    A history of controlling behavior and moral judgment.

    Long‑buried resentments that could fuel a sustained pattern of targeting particular kinds of women.

The emerging picture is of someone who feels entitled to punish or “correct” women they see as failing morally—cheaters, drinkers, or simply women who do not conform—while using staged accidents to hide their crimes.

Patrik’s colleagues, initially slower to see the pattern, eventually accept that the same hand has been at work nationally and now locally, and they focus on narrowing down who in this circle has the psychological profile, access and opportunity to orchestrate the drunk‑driving murders.
Revelation: Serial Killer and TV Murderer

Bit by bit, the police assemble enough evidence to confront the killer behind the staged car accidents. The reveal shows that:

    The “accidents” across Sweden, including Marit’s, were deliberate murders carried out or arranged by this person.

    Their motive ties back to personal, often misogynistic judgement and to an old, formative event that set their hatred and sense of justification in motion.

The killer’s identity is embedded in local respectability, which is why they evaded suspicion for so long: their social position and outward behavior did not fit the stereotype of a serial murderer. When exposed, the decades of hidden violence stand in stark contrast to their public role, and the guilt extends to those who, knowingly or not, enabled them by accepting the drunk‑driving explanations.​

Separately, the reality‑show murder is solved as a more “ordinary” crime of passion and resentment. The culprit is one of the contestants or people directly involved with the show, driven by personal hatred, jealousy or a desire to get rid of a rival. The case hinges on reconstructing movements during the drunken party, breaking down alibis, and sifting what is genuine from what was performed for the cameras.

Crucially, the TV murder and the serial‑killer case intersect mainly by coincidence of time and place rather than by a shared mastermind: Patrik realizes that while the town is dealing with two murders at once, they arise from different roots.
Emotional Fallout and Ending Notes

In the aftermath, the emotional consequences are heavy. Sophie, Marit’s daughter, and Marit’s lover must process that Marit was not a reckless drunk but a victim of a long‑running, calculated predator; their grief is mixed with relief that her name is cleared. Families of older victims across Sweden now learn that their loved ones were murdered, not simply careless drivers, reopening wounds but also offering a kind of bitter justice.

For Patrik and his colleagues, the case reinforces both their strengths and their flaws: they took too long to make certain “obvious” connections, but their persistence finally brought a serial killer to light. Patrik’s sensitivity to vulnerable witnesses, especially Sophie, stands out, and his stubborn doubt about the “simple” explanation is what cracked the pattern.

On the personal side, Erica and Patrik, despite stress, manage to keep their relationship intact and move forward with their marriage, keeping the series’ emotional core in place. Anna, still living with Erica, gains more stability and support, but her trauma continues to shape her choices, setting up threads for future books. The novel closes with the community scarred by the twin shocks of a reality‑show killing and the exposure of a respectable townsperson as a serial murderer, while Patrik heads into his marriage with Erica carrying both the weight of the case and the fragile hope of a normal family life.
